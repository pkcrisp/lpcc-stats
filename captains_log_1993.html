Sat 25 Sep 1993 🧢 1st XIv HC Hastings & St Leonards Priory CC · AwayformDDWLL

From the captain's chair — Saturday 25 September 1993. We travelled to Hastings & St Leonards Priory CC, lost the toss, and were sent out into the field, which on reflection may have suited us rather well.

They made 184/6 from their 48 overs, built chiefly around a fine fifty from Barry Dixon (55) and useful support from B. Phillips (47) and an unbeaten 30 from Peter Finch, the pair adding a crucial 76 for the second wicket that threatened to get away from us. For a spell there — 160/3 sliding to 172/6 — the floodgates opened and it looked rather ominous, but credit to the lads for keeping their heads. Jon Harris was magnificent with the ball, 4-24 and the architect of the collapse, accounting among others for three ducks (Carl Read, N Davies and Tim Morgan all departing without troubling the scorers), while Rob Doick chipped in with 1-34.

Chasing 185, David Castle played the innings of the day, 73 well past his fifty, ably assisted by Dave Charlton (26) and Piers McDonald (24), the openers putting on 52 to set the tone. We got home on 186/6 with a few overs to spare, though I'll confess our wicketkeeper had an afternoon to forget, twelve byes going begging behind the stumps — words were had, gently, over tea.

A fine win by 4 wickets, and with Nigel Thirkell still striding clear at the top of the run charts and Jon Harris now up to 49 wickets for the season, the 1st XI machine rolls on. Onwards.

Sat 25 Sep 1993 🧢 2nd XIv YC Yalding CC · HomeformDDWWD

As the summer of 1993 wraps up, honorary opening the ledger:

Skipper's Log, 25 September 1993: Yalding CC visited, I won the toss, and there was never much doubt what I'd do with it — a decent strip and a settled dressing room meant batting first was the only sensible course. Mike Elworthy played the innings of the day, motoring past his fifty to 61, while Dave Todd (39) and Ron McCormick (21) filled in the gaps either side of him. The openers deserve the real credit though — 105 for the first wicket is the kind of platform captains dream about and rarely get, and it took the shine off Trevor Back's afternoon somewhat, undone for a duck while the scoreboard ticked merrily on around him. We closed on 147/5 from our 42, a total that felt like enough without ever feeling like plenty.

So it proved. Yalding's reply never found its rhythm — 45/2 became 54/5 in the blink of an eye, and Liam Hawkins, M Ashton and D Shrimpton all trooped back without so much as a run to show for it. Don Dansie was the chief architect, 4-20 with three maidens thrown in for good measure — miserly barely covers it — with Dave Todd (2-6) and Chris Tymms (1-13) tightening the noose. I even chipped in with 39 myself, more by accident than design.

Yalding scrapped to 78/8 from their 29 before somehow escaping with a draw. Honours shared, then — though on the balance of an afternoon like that, we'll happily settle for calling it a win in all but name.

Wed 22 Sep 1993 🧢 Midweek XIv Penshurst Park CC · AwayformLWDDL

Wednesday 22 September 1993, and off we trundled to Penshurst Park, blessed with a coin toss that actually went our way for once — I had no hesitation in batting first.

What followed was rather splendid. Nigel Thirkell played the innings of a man born to bat on Penshurst's outfield, striking a magnificent 115, while Paul Bowles chipped in with a classy 55 alongside him — the pair adding 135 for the second wicket and doing most of my captaincy for me. We closed on 221 for 5 from our 38 overs, a total I was rather proud to defend, and a fitting stage for Nigel to bring up 27,000 career runs for the club — not a milestone one reaches by accident.

Defending it, however, proved less straightforward than the scorebook suggests. We had them 6 for 1 and dreaming of an early bath, but C Williamson (67) and Simon Leegood (50) stitched together a stubborn 122 for the third wicket that had me checking my watch rather too often, with John Darby's unbeaten 24 threatening to see them home. Thankfully Piers McDonald had other ideas, spinning his way to a magical 5 for 45, John Hicks fell for a golden duck, and Fred Cheesman (2-48) and the miserly Darren Styles (1-5, four maidens and not a run given away lightly) squeezed the last drops out of them. Penshurst finally folded for 210.

Eleven runs the margin, nerves thoroughly tested, but a very good day for the club indeed.

Sun 19 Sep 1993 🧢 1st XIv AC Anchorians CC · HomeformWDDWL

Sunday the nineteenth of September found Anchorians CC as our visitors, and having called correctly at the toss, I had no hesitation in choosing to bat — a decision that looked rather rash when we slipped to 1 for 1 before some of us had even loosened our shoulders. Defending that total, though, is where the wheels came off rather spectacularly. Keith Wooding and Gary Myers simply helped themselves, finishing unbeaten on 72 and 52 respectively as Anchorians cantered to 155 without loss in just 30.1 overs — a chase so serene it barely troubled the scorers. It didn't help that our man behind the stumps had an afternoon to forget, with 27 byes gifted along the way; catches may win matches, but so, evidently, do byes lose them.

Beaten by four wickets, then, and no arguing with the manner of it. Still, with 1106 runs to my name this season I can't complain too loudly, and we'll be better for this one come next Sunday.

Eighteen runs the wrong side of the ledger in the end. One bright note amid the wreckage: Ian Brattle's three wickets nudged him past John Taylor into 14th on the club's all-time list — some solace for the drive home. We'll be better for it next week.

I'll be delighted to hoy — David Castle carrying the innings, Alan Boyns cutting through their lower order. Let me write this up.

David Castle set about proceedings as if he had all afternoon, which, as it turned out, he very nearly did — 96 not out, the innings built squarely on his shoulders, with Clive Newman's fifty and Dave Charlton's 40 lending sturdy support. Alan Boyns led the charge with 3-9, Jon Harris chipped in 3-14 with four maidens of the miserly sort that sap a batting side's will to live, and Dave Todd took 2-36. Poor Chris Dockwray departed for a duck without so much as troubling the scorers, and High Halstow slid from 91-6 to 97 all out, Tony Wallace's battling 38 not out the only real resistance.

We won by 123 runs. And a word for the mantelpiece: David Castle brought up 10,000 career runs for the club during that innings — the sort of milestone that puts a lump in an old skipper's throat. A very good day indeed.

Wednesday last, we set off for The Mote in reasonably good spirits, which in hindsight was our first tactical error of the day. Lost the toss, got put in to field first, and I confess a small part of me was relieved to delay the reckoning with the bat.

Ten wickets is a proper hiding by anyone's measure, and there's no dressing it up. Heads up, though, lads — back to the nets, and better luck at the next fixture.
