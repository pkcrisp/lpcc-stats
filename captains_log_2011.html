🗓️ 24 Sep – 30 Sep 2011 · 2011 — 2 matches

Sun 25 Sep 2011🧢 1st XIv · HomeformLLWDL

We hosted the visitors on a fine Sunday, 25 September, and having called correctly at the toss, I had no hesitation in setting us in — the sort of decision that looks either shrewd or daft depending entirely on what follows, though for once I'll claim the credit.

The lads gave the bowlers a proper afternoon of it. Daniel Thirkell led from the front with a fine 83, well supported by Will Thirkell's 60 and Ryan Boyns' 50, the two of them putting on a crucial 79 for the third wicket while I fretted happily from the boundary. Poor Thomas Beeney was the one blot on an otherwise handsome copybook, done for a duck, but with 26 useful extras thrown in we posted a formidable 290/8 from our forty overs.

Defending it, we made early inroads and never quite let go, bowling the visitors out for 239 in the 39th over — Tom Davey's 71 the chief obstacle, with Adrian Samson and Adrian Moll chipping in, while Paul Davey and Ray Dixon both departed for ducks at our hands. Daniel Thirkell was superb with the ball too, 3-14, Martin Harris toiled manfully for 3-46 with two tidy maidens, and Stuart Cheesman took 1-5 as well as cracking 37 runs to move past Jim Hall into 21st on the club's all-time run-scorers list — no small feat. Ryan Boyns had a trickier time behind the stumps, 10 byes going astray.

And yet, for all that, we finished up on the wrong end of it, beaten by 51 runs. Not our day. Heads up, lads — back to the nets.

Sat 24 Sep 2011🧢 1st XIv Offham CC · HomeformWLLWD

Saturday 24 September 2011 saw us welcome Offham CC, and having won the toss I took one look at the pitch and fancied our chances with the willow. That confidence, in hindsight, was misplaced. Elsewhere in the ledger, Richard Piper continues his fine season, fourth in the 1st XI run charts on 294, while Matthew Carter leads the wickets with 28 — some consolation on an otherwise chastening day. Beaten by seven wickets, heads understandably down. Back to the nets, lads.

Wed 14 Sep 2011🧢 Midweek XIv Penshurst Park CC · AwayformDDDLW

As we always joked at Linton Park, winning the toss is half the battle — and losing it is the other half. Penshurst dug in for 198 all out off 39.2 overs, Andy Eames the chief architect with a tidy 52, John Boyden (39) and Graham Nicholls (34) filling in the mortar, and a stubborn 61-run stand for the eighth wicket that threatened to run away from us before Jon Harris (3-37) came steaming in to tidy things up. I even found some rhythm with the ball myself, picking up three. A special mention for Ryan, who brought up 2,000 career runs for the club along the way — a landmark worth more than any Wednesday scoreline.

A nervy finish, a scruffy start, but a win's a win: chalk this one up as a very good day for the club.
