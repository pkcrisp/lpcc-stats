Sat 27 Sep 1986 🧢 1st XIv HC Hastings & St Leonards Priory CC · HomeformLLLLD

Saturday 27th September 1986 saw Hastings & St Leonards Priory roll into Linton Park, and I duly lost the toss — a habit I'm beginning to suspect is less bad luck than a talent. They elected to bat, and we trooped into the field wondering what the afternoon held.

Nigel Thirkell had rather a lot to say about it, dismissing the openers cheaply and settling into a spell of such miserly accuracy — seven maidens! — that the Priory batsmen must have wondered whether the boundary rope had been quietly moved to the next parish. He finished with 3-23, well supported by Paul Gibson's 3-37 and a wicket for Paul Bowles, though Terry Wright (60) and Tim Booth-Jones (30) steadied things enough to see the visitors to 147/7 from their 51.2 overs — a total that felt gettable, if not gifted.

Our reply never quite found its stride. I managed to winkle out three wickets myself with the ball earlier, which I mention purely for the record and not at all because opportunities to boast are rare in this column. With the bat, though, poor Dave Todd contributed a golden duck to proceedings — these things happen to the best of us, Dave, and rather too often to the rest of us — and we stalled at 98/8 from 39 overs.

So honours shared, a draw when neither side could quite land the killer blow. Not a classic for the ages, perhaps, but a thoroughly honest afternoon's cricket — and with Nigel Thirkell topping our bowling and running charts alike, we remain in thoroughly capable hands.
