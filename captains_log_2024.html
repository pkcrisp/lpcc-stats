Sat 21 Sep 2024🧢 1st XIv Sissinghurst CC · AwayformWWWLL

We were sent out to Sissinghurst on the 21st of September full of end-of-season swagger, though the coin didn't share our enthusiasm and put us in the field first as they chose to bowl — sorry, batting. Never mind. No complaints when the lads then went out and put together 202 for 2 off their 35 overs, built almost entirely around a magnificent stand of 171 for the second wicket between Jordan Austin (53 not out) and Toby Joseph (50 not out), both bringing up well-made fifties while the rest of us admired from the boundary rope. Luke Welsman chipped in an unbeaten 38, and even the extras column got in on the act with a generous 28 — Sissinghurst's bowlers doing their bit for the cause, whether they meant to or not.

Defending it, we stumbled them early to 6 for 1, which felt like the perfect start until Ben Parsons (74 not out) and Philip Buckingham (30) stitched together 84 for the second wicket and threatened to spoil the afternoon. But 143 for 3 was as far as they got, thanks to Alex Wade's canny 1 for 11, Martin Harris chipping in with 1 for 29, and Farhan Malik strangling the middle overs with four maidens that gave away nothing but frustration.

Personal milestones sweetened the pot nicely: Luke Welsman raised 6,000 career runs for the club, and Jordan Austin's fifty vaulted him past Piers McDonald into 17th on the all-time list. A fine day out — 59 runs the margin, two points banked, and thoroughly, thoroughly earned.
