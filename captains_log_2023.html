Sat 23 Sep 2023 🧢 Friendly XIv Town Malling CC · HomeformLLWDL

We hosted Town Malling on a pleasant, grey Saturday afternoon, and having called correctly I did what any captain with a soft spot for runs on the board does — stuck us in.

It proved the right call. Benjamin Austin and Aaron Hughes put on 90 for the first wicket before either was so much as troubled, and the pair went on to finish unbeaten on 51 and 50 respectively, a fine advert for not getting carried away once you're in. Neill Pennefather chipped in with a brisk 36 to push us on to 186/8 from our 35 overs — though the less said about Euan Beardwell's contribution the better; a golden duck to add to the club's ever-growing ledger of shame.

With 186 to defend, the lads made short work of it. Town Malling never quite got going, and once they slid from 109-7 to all out for 112 in 33.1 overs, the contest was as good as done. Nolan Wilde led the attack beautifully with 2-25, Laurie Probert and Alex Wade picked up a wicket apiece, and Sam King bowled two miserly maidens that gave the batters nothing to work with. Daniel Barrett and Steve Sutton departed without scoring, evening the scales after our own top order's generosity.

A 74-run win, then, and a thoroughly satisfying afternoon's work — Aaron Hughes now tops our run charts for the season, and Tim Thirkell sits second among the wicket-takers. Onwards indeed.

Sun 17 Sep 2023 🧢 Friendly XIv BL Brockley CC, London · HomeformLLLWD

It was one of those September afternoons Kent does so well—warm, with a fine drizzle that never quite committed to rain—and I fancied it might do enough off the seam to have a look with the ball. Brockley CC had other ideas. Josh McDonald played beautifully for his 83, and once he and Anand Kumar had put on 110 for the second wicket, the writing was rather on the pavilion wall. Anand finished unbeaten on a well-made 58, Ritesh Ritesh chipped in with 56 of his own, and by the time thirty overs were up Brockley had racked up 235/4. Nolan Wilde was our pick of the attack with 2-30, toiling away manfully, and Martin Harris kept things honest with 1-44—though on this pitch, honest wasn't nearly enough.

Chasing 236 was always going to want something special, and while Aaron Hughes gave it a proper go with 37, well supported by Anthony Karassellos's 28, the required rate crept up like the drizzle and eventually swallowed us whole. All out for 130/7 told its own story.

Season-wise there are bright spots—Aaron leads our run charts with 184, Martin tops the wickets with 7—so not all is doom and gloom in the Linton Park camp. But today, beaten by 105 runs, there's no dressing it up: heads up, chins up, and back to the nets. Every dog has its day; this one, emphatically, belonged to Brockley.
