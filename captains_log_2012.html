Sat 22 Sep 2012🧢 1st XIv Detling CC · HomeformWDWWW

Detling CC came to Linton Park on Saturday 22nd, and winning the toss I had no hesitation in taking first knock — the right call, I still maintain, whatever the scorebook might suggest afterwards.

We huddled to 79/7 from our fifteen overs, which is the sort of total that makes a captain suddenly very interested in his shoelaces. Joshua Knowler top-scored with a gritty 20, well backed up by Matt Bramley-Harker's own 20, and between them they dragged us to something resembling respectability. Elsewhere in the order it was a story best told quietly — a nod to Nat Presneill, who made the long walk back for a duck and will be buying the teas for a fortnight.

Eighty wasn't going to trouble anyone, and so it proved. Detling eased to 80/4 inside their fifteen, Barry Town unbeaten on 35 doing the damage with Ian Thomas chipping in a handy 21. We did have our moments — Joshua Town back in the pavilion for nought at our hands, and Thomas Beeney bowling beautifully for 1-1, with Will Thew (1-4) and Will Thirkell (1-12) offering fine support, Thirkell continuing his fine season sitting third in both the club's run and wicket charts.

In the end, though, six wickets was too generous a margin to claw back. Not our day. Heads up, chins high, and back to the nets.

Sun 16 Sep 2012🧢 Sunday 1st XIv Cowdrey CC · HomeformLWWLW

Sunday the 16th of September saw Cowdrey CC roll into Linton Park, and having called correctly at the toss for once in my captaincy career, I had no hesitation whatsoever in sticking us in to bat. Glen Rawson set about proving why with a thoroughly well-made 82, finding a willing ally in Daniel Thirkell (39) and Tim Wright (37), the pair of them putting on 83 for the fourth wicket while the rest of us looked on approvingly from the boundary. Not everything gleamed, mind — Stuart Cheesman and Trevor Back both departed for ducks, contributing rather less to the scorebook than the extras column, which chipped in a generous 27. Even so, 202/6 from our forty overs felt a healthy total to defend.

Defend it we did. Cowdrey's top order wobbled early, 2-1 down inside a few overs, and though Glen Warner played a fine lone hand of 55, a 52-run fifth-wicket stand was as good as it got for the visitors before Will Thirkell ran through them with 3-16, Richard Knowler chipping in with 2-1 off three miserly maidens, and Martin Harris mopping up with 2-36. David Challis, Robert Fenwick and Will Needham all went for nought, and Cowdrey folded for 153 in 39.1 overs. Poor Daniel Thirkell had a torrid afternoon behind the stumps, 15 byes conceded, but nobody remembers that with a 49-run win in the book — and a hearty round of applause for Richard Knowler, who brought up 200 career wickets in some style. A fine result, and long may it continue.
