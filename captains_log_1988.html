🗓️ 01 Oct – 07 Oct 1988 · 1988 — 1 match

Sat 01 Oct 1988 🧢 1st XIv HC Hastings & St Leonards Priory CC · HomeformWWDLL

Lost the toss, and asked to bowl — which on a fresh Saturday in October always sounds like a raw deal until you remember somebody has to graft through the middle overs. Hastings & St Leonards Priory made the most of batting first, recovering from an early wobble at 6 for 1 to post 196 for 6 off their 45.2 overs, built on Keith Turk's watchful 40, with I Phillips (29) and Ian Gillespie (28) chipping in and a stubborn last-wicket stand of 50 that we could frankly have done without. Matt Large did the heavy lifting with 2 for 46 and four miserly maidens, well backed by Simon Hammond (2-62) and David Castle (1-41).

Chasing 197 was always going to ask questions of our top order, and while Nigel Thirkell (37) and Tim Thirkell (31) put on 83 for the third wicket to suggest we might just get away with it, the innings unravelled in that maddening way club cricket specialises in — 117 for 6 becoming 129 for 9 almost before anyone had finished their tea. Paul Sendles and Stewart Morrison both fell without troubling the scorers, and your captain contributed a battling 37 of his own for what it was worth. David Castle's afternoon behind the stumps came at the cost of 8 byes.

Beaten by 67 runs, then — no disgrace against a side that batted with real application, but there's plenty for us to tidy up. We'll be better for it next week.

Wed 28 Sep 1988 🧢 Midweek XIv Penshurst Park CC · AwayformLLDWD

Wednesday 28th September, and out we trundled to Penshurst Park CC with the coin doing us a favour for once — I called it right and took first use of what looked a decent strip. We were duly bowled out for 143 in the 40th over, which felt a touch light, but Nigel Thirkell batted with real purpose for his 57, well backed up by brother Tim's 29. Less said the better about the middle order, where Peter Whyte and Jon Harris both took the long walk back without troubling the scorer — the Duck Club ledger swells again.

With the ball, we set about defending the total and for a good while it looked a contest. Simon Hammond led from the front with 2-27, Nigel Thirkell chipped in again with 1-21, Jon Harris took 1-31, and Ian Brattle bowled two tidy maidens to keep things honest. But Penshurst's third-wicket pair had other ideas, adding 90 between them as Grayland (52) and John Hyde (50) both brought up well-made fifties, and the total edged past us at 144-5 in the 36th over.

As for your correspondent — a duck, and a rather tame one at that. We move on. Nigel Thirkell remains the season's shining light, top of both run and wicket charts, and long may that continue. Beaten by five wickets, no disgrace in it, and we'll be sharper next week.

Sat 10 Sep 1988 🧢 1st XIv SC Southborough CC · AwayformWDLWW

From the captain's chair — Saturday 10 September 1988. We travelled to Southborough CC, the sun more or less behaving itself, and I won the toss and, in a rare fit of decisiveness, stuck us in to bat.

What followed was the kind of innings that makes all those cold April nets sessions feel worthwhile. Brian Piper and Paul Bowles put on 215 for the first wicket before anyone troubled the scorebook with a dismissal, Piper going on to a magnificent 102 and Bowles falling agonisingly short with 97. Paul Gibson finished unbeaten on 52 to help us declare, in effect, at a formidable 280 for 2 from our 46 overs — extras chipping in a generous 25, presumably while I fretted in the pavilion doing sums on the back of a scorecard.

With that total in the bank, the bowling looked after itself. Southborough rallied through Peter Young's fighting 77 and a handy stand of 91 for the fourth wicket, but Ian Brattle was in destructive form with 3 for 37, well backed by Alan Boyns, Paul Gibson and a typically parsimonious Matt Large. Poor Jim Hall had a torrid afternoon behind the stumps, 12 byes going begging, while I can't pretend my own 97 didn't feel rather satisfying.

Nice touches too: Gibson reaching 100 career wickets, and Piper's century easing him past Jim Hall into 10th on the all-time run-scorers' list. We ran out winners by 113 runs — a thoroughly good day for the club, and one for the scrapbook.
