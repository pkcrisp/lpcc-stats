🗓️ 28 Sep – 04 Oct 2002 · 2002 — 1 match

Sat 28 Sep 2002 🧢 1st XIv BC Bidborough CC · HomeformLLLWL

Saturday 28 September 2002 saw us welcome Bidborough CC, and having won the toss I elected to bat — the right call, if I say so myself, and for once I'll take the credit before the lads take it off me. We rattled along to a healthy 188/6 from our 40 overs, built on two handsome half-centuries: I was delighted to lead from the front with 66, while Clive Newman chipped in with a classy 65 alongside me. Not every innings can be a fairy tale, mind — poor Rob Jackson's duck was the one blot on an otherwise rosy scorecard, though he'd have his revenge later.

In the field we gave a good account of ourselves without quite closing the door. Bidborough's R. Warner played the innings of the day, an assured 58, well supported by G. Gask (29) and Gary Thorne (28), and they eventually got home at 191/6 with just five balls of their 40 overs to spare. Credit where it's due: Rob Jackson bowled beautifully for 2-15 — a fine riposte to his golden duck — with Jon Harris and Darren Styles picking up a wicket apiece, and Mark Christmas keeping things admirably tight with two maidens.

In the end, four wickets was the margin, and no amount of after-match cake could quite sweeten that one. Clive Newman continues to top our run charts for the season with 886, and Michael Beeney leads the wickets with 37 — reminders, at least, that the summer's been kinder to us than this particular Saturday. We'll be better for it next week.

Wed 25 Sep 2002 🧢 Midweek XIv DS Dummy Site · AwayformDLWLW

Wednesday last, we took ourselves off to Dummy Site, and for once I won the toss — a rare enough event that I nearly forgot what to do with it. Batting first seemed the sensible course, and 217 for 7 from our forty overs was a tidy return. Michael Beeney played the anchor beautifully for a well-made 71, with Craig Sands (45 not out) and Trevor Back (32) lending sturdy support. Less gloriously, Mike Elworthy and Mark Christmas both took the long walk back for nought — the Duck Club welcomes two new members, and no doubt they'll hear about it over tea for weeks to come.

In the field we gave everything we had. Their innings closed on 203 for 8, with Connelly's 49 the backbone of the chase, backed by Mitchell (40) and Exton (30). Mark Christmas was excellent with the ball, 3 for 38, and found willing accomplices in Trevor Back (1-20) and Michael Beeney (1-24).

There was proper cause for cheer amid the disappointment: Michael Beeney brought up 1,000 career runs for the club, a landmark worth raising a glass to. Trevor Back and Mark Christmas continue to sit second in the Midweek XI's season charts for runs and wickets respectively — form that bodes well for the rest of the campaign.

In the end, though, we fell short by 14 runs. Honest effort, honestly beaten. We'll dust ourselves down and come back stronger next week.
