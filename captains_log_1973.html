🗓️ 29 Sep – 05 Oct 1973 · 1973 — 1 match

Sat 29 Sep 1973 🧢 1st XIv BC Bexhill CC · HomeformDLLLD

Beaten by the toss and then, twelve runs later, beaten in the middle - Saturday's visit from Bexhill CC was one of those games where you feel the result was decided before a ball was bowled. Put in the field, we did our bit: Bexhill limped to 117/9 off their 48.3 overs, with P Shrubb's 24 the top score and honest support from Andy King (21) and Dave Cowley (20). John Thirkell was the pick of the attack with 2-8, Andrew Moss and Tony Gauna chipped in with two apiece, and Nigel Thirkell bowled five maidens of the sort that make batsmen question their life choices.

A gettable 118 to win, we thought - and so it should have been. Andrew Moss batted with real purpose for 30, Ken Webb supported nicely with 23, but the rest of us rather lost the plot and we folded for 105 in 32 overs. Nigel Thirkell, having bowled like a dream, matched it with a golden duck at the other end - the game does love its little ironies.

There was, at least, cause for a raised glass: Tony Gauna's two wickets edged him past Stan Wratten into 20th on the club's all-time wicket-takers list, while Paul Brattle (2nd in this year's run charts) and Nigel Thirkell (2nd in the wickets) continue to fly the flag for 1973.

Twelve runs the margin, and no complaints - we simply didn't do enough with the bat. We'll regroup, and we'll be better for it next week.

Sun 23 Sep 1973 🧢 1st XIv AC Anchorians CC · HomeformLDLLL

Sunday's fixture against Anchorians CC was billed as a battle, and it very nearly delivered one — except I lost the toss again, which by now is less bad luck than a personality trait. They chose to bat, and set about their 44 overs with quiet purpose, posting 136/6. E. Cherry led the way with a tidy 34, well supported by Derek Ufton (23) and Alan Dixon (22), while our attack chipped away throughout. Nigel Thirkell was the pick of the bowlers by some distance — 4-34 with five maidens thrown in for good measure, proof that not a run was given away lightly. Tony Gauna and Allan Cooper each took a wicket apiece to keep the visitors honest.

Chasing 137, we made light work of it in the end. Paul Brattle and Paul Bowles simply refused to be parted, carrying us to 78/1 from 19 overs with unbeaten knocks of 35 and 32 respectively — the sort of composed batting that makes a captain's job look easy, even when it wasn't him doing any of the composing.

A nod, too, to the record books: Tony Gauna's solitary wicket nudged him past Len Conway into 21st on the club's all-time list, while Paul Brattle continues to sit 2nd in this season's run charts on 555, and Nigel Thirkell holds 2nd in the wicket charts with 51.

In the end honours were shared, neither side quite able to force the issue. A draw, then — honest, hard-fought, and entirely fitting for a Sunday when both attack and bat had their say.

Not our day, then — down by two wickets in a match we really ought to have batted better in. Heads up, and back to the nets.
