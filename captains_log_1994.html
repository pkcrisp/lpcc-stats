Wed 28 Sep 1994 🧢 Midweek XIv Penshurst Park CC · AwayformDWDDD

We won by five runs at Penshurst Park, batting first to 213/5 and defending it despite a nervy finish.

Here's the report:

Wednesday last saw us motoring down to Penshurst Park with the sun out and, rather more importantly, the coin landing my way. I had no hesitation in sticking us in, and for once the boys made me look like a genius rather than a fool. Mike Caddick played the innings of a man determined to remind the league who tops the run charts, cracking a fine 71, while Nigel Thirkell chipped in a classy 54 and Peter Whyte a tidy 27. The 104-run stand for the second wicket was the platform on which the whole innings was built, and 213/5 from our 47 overs felt like a total with a bit of bite to it.

Defending it proved rather more of a white-knuckle affair than any of us fancied. Penshurst were reduced to 4-1 early doors, which was promising, but a stubborn 69-run third-wicket stand threatened to drag the game away from us, John Boyden (71 not out) and R Hunter (51) doing most of the damage. Step forward Ian Brattle, whose 5-66 — three of those overs maidens, no less — was the difference between a comfortable evening and a nail-biting one. Alan Boyns backed him up admirably with 2-19, though his afternoon with the gloves was less serene, nine byes going astray. My own 54 kept me honest, and three ducks — Webb, Banks and Lock — kept the scorebook cheerful.

Five runs. That'll do nicely.

Sat 24 Sep 1994 🧢 1st XIv HC Hastings & St Leonards Priory CC · HomeformWWLWL

The toss deserted us before a ball was bowled, and so it was into the field we trooped on a Saturday that promised more than it delivered. But cricket, and this club in particular, does love a twist: from a healthy 155-3 we contrived to stumble to 170-6 in the blink of an eye, Don Dansie's unbeaten 28 the only calm in the chaos. We fell short on 175-6, twelve runs shy.

Not our day, then. Heads up, chins out, back to the nets — Thirkell's runs and Harris's wickets keep the season charts glowing even when the results don't.

Sat 24 Sep 1994 🧢 2nd XIv YC Yalding CC · AwayformWDWLL

One shaft of light: Trevor's five-for nudges him past Ian Brattle into 12th on the club's all-time wicket list, while Mike Elworthy and Alan Boyns continue to lead the 2nd XI's season charts. Small mercies. Beaten by 99 runs — heads up, and back to the nets.

Sat 17 Sep 1994 🧢 1st XIv West Farleigh CC · HomeformWWWLW

From the captain's chair — Saturday 17 September 1994, and a home fixture against West Farleigh CC on a day that, in the end, did us proud, whatever the scorebook's closing line might suggest.

Winning the toss, I had no hesitation in asking the lads to bat first, and it very nearly went swimmingly. But cometh the hour, cometh Paul Bowles, who compiled a magnificent unbeaten century, and in tandem with Nigel Thirkell's fine 36 put on 115 for the second wicket to drag us to a highly respectable 163 for 2 off our 44.2 overs. Don Dansie's brisk 20 not out rounded things off nicely.

Alas, defending it proved beyond us. West Farleigh lost an early wicket cheaply enough to raise hopes, but R Peterson and Dave Latter shared 84 for the second wicket and never looked back, Peterson finishing unbeaten on a splendid 71 and Latter falling just short of his own century of hospitality with 58. Darren Styles and Chris Gray toiled honestly — Gray's two maidens a small mercy in a chase that reached 166 for 2 with overs to spare. I did my modest bit with 36, and Nigel Thirkell continues to top the season's run charts regardless.

Beaten by eight wickets, then — but with Bowles in this sort of form, we'll take some stopping yet.

Sun 04 Sep 1994 🧢 Tour XIv BC Beacon CC, Cornwall · AwayformWWLLW

Sunday 4th September brought us to Beacon CC in Cornwall, a fixture that always promises sea air, cream teas, and the possibility of losing all sense of the scoreboard. I won the toss and had no hesitation in batting first — a decision I felt rather smug about at the time, and one the lads didn't let me forget either way, whatever happened.

We posted 152 for 7 from our forty overs, a total built almost single-handedly by Nigel Thirkell, whose 69 was the innings' backbone — a proper well-made fifty and then some. A useful 59-run stand for the fourth wicket gave the innings some spine, though I'll confess it never quite felt like enough as we walked off.

It nearly wasn't. Beacon replied with real intent, Chris Bennett top-scoring with a brisk 46, but our attack found its rhythm: David Castle was superb with 2 for 21, Nigel Thirkell chipped in again with 2 for 26 — a fine all-round day's work — and Richard Ashby snaffled one for the princely cost of a single run. They were bowled out for exactly 152 in 23.3 overs.

Level. Dead level. Twenty-two men, a whole day's toil, and not a run between us. You couldn't script it, and frankly I wouldn't want to — some results are better left as they fell. A tie is nobody's triumph and everybody's story, and that's precisely how it should stay down at Beacon.
