Sun 28 Nov 2021 🧢 Friendly XIv Bearsted CC · HomeformLWWLW

Sunday 28 November brought a bitter, blustery afternoon to Linton Park, the sort of day where the toss feels like the only warm moment you'll get, and Bearsted CC arrived to find us in no mood to hang about. Batting first, we cracked on to 107/2 from a brisk nine overs, with Aaron Hughes (26 not out), Sam Boyns (26 not out) and Luke Welsman (25 not out) all sharing the scoring duties as if divvying up a round at the bar — nobody greedy, everybody contributing.

With the ball, things got even better. Bearsted's reply folded to 64 all out in just 7.3 overs, Christian Roberts's 20 the only real resistance, while poor Lewis Bedwell trudged off for a golden duck without so much as a single to his name. Anthony Karassellos was the chief destroyer with 3-30, backed up nicely by Sam Boyns's 1-9 — a lad clearly determined to have his say with bat and ball both. I even chipped in with 26 myself, purely in a supporting role, you understand.

Beyond the scorebook, today had a touch of history: Benjamin Austin reached 3,000 career runs for the club, and Luke Welsman brought up 4,000 — proper landmarks on a proper cold day. Aaron Hughes continues to sit third in the Friendly XI run charts, with Karassellos still leading the wickets column.

We ran out winners by 43 runs. Cold hands, warm hearts, good day for the club.

Sun 10 Oct 2021 🧢 1st XIv The Mote CC · AwayformLWWWL

Skipper's Log, Sunday 10 October 2021 — away to The Mote CC.

Won the toss, and there was never really a debate in my head about what to do with it. Bat first, ask questions later. We laboured to 93/5 from our nine overs, which on reflection was always going to leave us needing something special with the ball. Sam Boyns held the innings together with an unbeaten 26, Luke Welsman chipped in nicely with 23, and I managed 26 myself, proving even the captain can occasionally hold a bat the right way up. Special mention, sadly, to poor Anthony Karassellos, who departed for a duck without troubling the scorers — these things happen to the best of us, Anthony, chin up.

With the ball, we simply couldn't find a way through. The Mote raced to 97/2 in just 7.1 overs, Matthew White (25 not out) and Theeshan Satkunaseelan (25 not out) doing the early damage with a stand of 53, before Matt Sadler (21 not out) finished the job off in a hurry. Sam Boyns was our pick of the bowlers with 1-17, but one good over — the fifth, where we did claim two wickets — was very much the exception rather than the rule.

Elsewhere, a word of cheer: Luke Welsman continues his fine season, sitting third in the 1st XI run charts with 226 for the year.

Not our day, beaten by four wickets. Heads up, and back to the nets — we'll live to fight another Sunday.

Sat 25 Sep 2021 🧢 Friendly XIv SC Selling CC · AwayformWLWWL

Skipper's Log, Saturday 25 September 2021 — away at Selling CC on a warm, overcast afternoon perfect for putting one's feet up, though as it turned out we'd need every over.

I called correctly at the toss, which around these parts counts as the highlight of most seasons, and had no hesitation whatsoever in choosing to bat. Craig Sands then went and reminded everyone why: 147, three figures and no less, an innings that also carried him past Paul Gibson into 7th on the club's all-time run-scorers list and over the 16,000-run mark for his career — a fine afternoon's work by anyone's reckoning. Richard Skye (71 not out) and Anthony Karassellos (51) played handsomely in support, while poor Nolan Wilde contributed a golden duck, the sort of nothing that makes for excellent tea-time ribbing. We closed on 304/5 from our 40, a total that felt, at the time, faintly untouchable.

Selling had other ideas, replying with 301/8 from their 40 — Brad Wretham (78) doing most of the damage, well supported by Tom Hinkes (46) and Nick Myers (36 not out) — and for a good while it looked touch and go. Anthony Karassellos kept his head to finish with 2-29, backed up by Aaron Hughes (2-31) and John Karassellos (2-49), and it was he who also brought up his 100th career wicket for the club along the way. He now leads our 2021 Friendly XI wicket charts with six, as Craig does the run charts with 147.

A tense finish, but a win is a win, and by 3 runs it was a famous one. Onwards.
