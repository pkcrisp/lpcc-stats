Sun 19 Sep 1976 🧢 1st XIv AC Anchorians CC · HomeformWWWDW

Sunday 19 September saw Anchorians travel to us at Linton Park, and winning the toss I had no hesitation in taking first knock — a decision I'm rather pleased to report vindicated itself handsomely. We posted 202 for 5 from our forty overs, built around a wonderful hundred from Paul Bowles, who middled it beautifully for 101. Nigel Thirkell chipped in with a fine 56, and Paul Brattle's 21 added the sort of late impetus that turns a good total into a defendable one.

With the ball, I confess I rather fancied our chances, and so it proved. Anchorians were bowled out for 155 in 40.4 overs, J Coppins battling hardest with 40, Alan Dixon adding 38 and S Tapping 28, but the middle and lower order simply couldn't build partnerships against our attack — poor E-Cherry departed for a duck without so much as a glimpse of the scoreboard. Paul Bowles, not content with his century, ran through them with 4 for 11, Trevor Back chipped away for 3 for 9, and Tony Gauna bowled a tidy line for 1 for 9 with two maidens thrown in for good measure. I managed 4 wickets myself, which I shall modestly describe as leading from the front twice in one afternoon.

A word for the history books: Paul Bowles's century sees him leapfrog Stan Wratten into 4th on the club's all-time run-scorers list, and he continues to top our 1976 run charts with 1381, with Nigel Thirkell leading the wickets column on 89.

A comfortable 47-run win, and a thoroughly satisfying way to spend a September Sunday. Onwards.

Sat 18 Sep 1976 🧢 1st XIv West Farleigh CC · HomeformDWWWD

From the captain's chair — Saturday 18 September 1976. We hosted West Farleigh CC on a fine afternoon, and having called correctly at the toss, I had no hesitation whatsoever in sticking us in to bat. Batting first is always the captain's little vanity project, and for once it paid handsome dividends.

Paul Bowles made an absolute mockery of the West Farleigh attack with a superb 104, the sort of innings that has the tea urn practically applauding itself, while Paul Brattle chipped in with a stout 42 in support. We closed on 178/3 from our 45 overs, a total that felt, even to my cautious captain's eye, rather more than useful.

With the ball, the lads set about their work with real purpose. Jon Harris led from the front with 4-68, Nigel Thirkell was miserliness personified with 2-15 and five maidens to his name, and Martin Mears weighed in with 2-18. Don Fermor (36) and Brian Piper (31) threatened to make a match of it for the visitors, with Peter Cooper adding 20, but Trevor Fermor's golden duck rather summed up their afternoon. I even found some rhythm myself, taking four wickets, though poor Jim Hall had a torrid time behind the stumps, ten byes slipping through his gloves like eels. West Farleigh were all out for 160 from 50 overs.

A win by 18 runs, then, and a thoroughly satisfying one. Paul Bowles continues to lead our run charts for the season, Nigel Thirkell the wickets — long may both continue. Onwards.
