Wed 30 Sep 1987 🧢 Midweek XIv Penshurst Park CC · AwayformDLDWD

Wednesday 30th September saw us make the trip to Penshurst Park, and having called correctly for once in my captaincy career, there was never much doubt about what I'd do with it — up we went to bat.

What followed was as handsome a display as this scribe has had the pleasure of witnessing. The openers set the tone with 95 for the first wicket before either fell, and then David Castle simply took over, compiling a majestic 100 not out while Andrew Hales chipped in with a classy 64 and the pair added 110 for the second wicket. Paul Bowles' 35 rounded things off nicely as we closed on 206 for 2 from our 43.3 overs — the kind of total that makes the tea interval taste that bit sweeter.

Defending it was another matter entirely. Penshurst's Milns played a fine hand of 52, well supported by Darby (34) and Boyden (26), and a stand of 66 for the third wicket had us shifting in our boots. But cricket has a way of turning, and turn it did — they slid from 182 for 6 to 192 for 9, with G Cooper and John Lambert both back in the hutch without so much as a run to their name. Ian Brattle was magnificent, snaring 4 for 53, with Bowles (3-23) and Thirkell (1-10) offering fine support. My own contribution behind the stumps was rather less distinguished — 11 byes is not a figure I'll be framing.

A tense finish, but a fine win by 10 runs. Onwards indeed.

Sun 27 Sep 1987 🧢 1st XIv DS Dummy Site · HomeformDWDDD

The lads gathered on Sunday, all set for the visit of Dummy Site — and true to form, I lost the toss again, watching them elect to bat while I tried to look unbothered about it.

They ground their way to 158 all out in 57.4 overs, with Titchener-Barrett unbeaten on 34 and Allen (27) and Oldridge (26) chipping in respectably enough. For a while it looked ominous — 56-2 sliding to 70-5 in a hurry — before Jon Harris got among them properly, 4-46 with six miserly maidens thrown in for good measure, ably assisted by Alan Boyns (3-22) and Malcolm Pay (2-5). Poor Irwin and Jefferson trudged back without so much as troubling the scorers, which is never a pleasant walk.

Our reply was a rather more nervous affair. Two down for one run and I confess I did wonder what I'd say in this very report. But Keith Hatcher, calm as you like, played a lovely knock of 58, with David Castle (36) and Bob Lines (23) lending sturdy support — the third-wicket pair adding a priceless 73 before the middle order rather lost its head, 119-3 becoming 128-6 in the blink of an eye. Whyte, Simmonds and Large all made their way back for ducks, bless them, and we finished on 139-8. Castle also had a busy afternoon behind the stumps, 20 byes the price of an entertaining contest — though he can console himself as our leading run-scorer this season, just as Harris tops the wicket charts, and reached 1500 career victims in the process.

A draw, then — honours even, and on the balance of the day's fortunes, a fair result all round.

Sat 26 Sep 1987 🧢 1st XIv HC Hastings & St Leonards Priory CC · HomeformLDWDD

As the toss went against us and we were put in the field, I'll say straight away it was the right decision by our visitors, even if I'd never admit that in the tea queue. Peter Whyte, meanwhile, joined Terry in the Duck Club for the day — misery loves company.

A draw, then — honours shared, pride just about intact, and Jon Harris continuing his magnificent season with the ball. Not a classic, but an honest afternoon's cricket, and we'll take that.

Sun 20 Sep 1987 🧢 1st XIv AC Anchorians CC · HomeformLLDWD

From the captain's chair, Sunday 20 September 1987. We welcomed Anchorians CC, lost the toss, and were put out into the field — never my favourite way to spend a Sunday, but there it is.

Graham Siveyer batted as though he owned the square, cruising to 96 not out, while Keith Wooding chipped in a handsome 80, the pair putting on 179 for the second wicket and leaving our bowlers rather short of answers. Anchorians closed on 217/2 from their 42 overs. Credit where due: Matt Large kept plugging away for 2-42, and Alan Boyns was miserliness personified with three maidens.

Chasing 218, we made a fist of it. Nigel Thirkell played the innings of the day, 89 well past his fifty, ably backed by David Castle's 40, and the openers laid a fine platform of 93 for the first wicket. Then, as is our club's habit, the wheels came off spectacularly — 141-3 became 146-6 in the blink of an eye, with Simon Thirkell, Tim Thirkell and Ian McKechnie all failing to trouble the scorers. Your correspondent also made a duck; the less said, the better. We finished 149/9 from 43 overs.

Honours were shared. But the day belonged to David Castle, marking his 100th appearance in Linton colours and climbing past Robert Chance to 22nd on the all-time run list — a thoroughly deserved landmark on a day when little else went entirely our way.

Sat 19 Sep 1987 🧢 1st XIv West Farleigh CC · AwayformWLLDW

Saturday 19th September, and the coin toss went as all coin tosses seem to go for us this year — badly. West Farleigh won the call, elected to bat, and left us to spend the afternoon leathering the outfield instead of enjoying it.

To our credit, the lads gave them precious little to smile about. Jon Harris was magnificent, prising out five for 51 in a spell that had their middle order in all sorts of bother, while Nigel Thirkell and David Castle chipped in with a wicket apiece to keep the pressure on. Matt Large, meanwhile, bowled with the miserly discipline of a man who simply refuses to be hit, three maidens to his name. For all that, Mick Holness played the innings of the day with 42, well supported by Mick Beeney's 29 and Tim Baker's 28, and West Farleigh eventually declared themselves satisfied on 156 for 9 from their 48.3 overs.

Our reply amounted to six for none from three overs before, in the finest traditions of the game, both captains shook hands and agreed there was no more cricket to be had in it. Honours shared, then — a draw that suited nobody and offended nobody.

Elsewhere in the ledger, David Castle continues to sit proudly second in our 1987 run charts with 1246 to his name, and Jon Harris now leads the wicket-takers with 89 — form he showed no sign of hiding today. A fair result, if not a thrilling one: we came, we bowled rather well, and we left with a draw and our dignity largely intact.

Wed 09 Sep 1987 🧢 Midweek XIv The Mote CC · HomeformDDLDW

Here's the report:

---

Wednesday 9 September, and The Mote CC rolled into Linton Park under skies that, for once, seemed to favour cricket. I called correctly at the toss — a rare enough event that I nearly forgot what to do next — and had no hesitation in batting first.

The lads batted with real purpose. Nigel Thirkell was in imperious touch, finishing unbeaten on a chanceless 80, while David Castle chipped in with a fine 51 and Paul Gibson added a brisk 37. The Mote didn't make life easy in reply. Their openers put on 105 together — the sort of start that has a fielding captain reaching for his cap and rearranging his field for the tenth time — before Jon Harris, bowling beautifully for 3 for 52, finally prised them apart. Malcolm Bristow (64) and Brian Piper (53) kept them in the hunt, but Nigel Thirkell, Trevor Back and I all found a wicket apiece, and Alan Boyns bowled miserly enough to send down seven maidens. They closed on 176 for 7.

A draw, then — honours shared, and nobody quite able to land the decisive blow. Special mention to Paul Gibson, who brought up 3,000 career runs for the club along the way. A thoroughly good day's cricket, even if my captaincy contributed rather less than the toss coin did.

Beaten by 3 wickets, but I'll say this for the lads: we made Bearsted work for it.

Credit where it's due: Matt Large atoned in style with 4-33, Sean Charlton and Allan Cooper chipped in, and Jon Harris bowled six overs as tight as his club-leading wicket tally suggests.

In the end, 144/7 in 54 overs edged us out by three wickets. Not our finest hour, but there was enough good cricket in there to suggest better days are coming — and coming soon, I hope.
