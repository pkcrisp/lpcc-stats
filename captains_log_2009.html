Sat 26 Sep 2009 🧢 1st XIv CC Chelsfield CC · HomeformLWLLL

Saturday 26 September 2009, and Chelsfield CC rolled up for what turned out to be a proper day's cricket. I lost the toss — no great surprise there, my coin-calling remains as reliable as an English summer — and we were sent out to bowl.

Chelsfield made a fair fist of things, posting 181 all out from their 42 overs. Howard was the chief architect, an unbeaten 87 that did the bulk of the damage, with Robertson (32) and Ross (30) chipping in nicely. Credit to us, though: U Hassan and Miles Walker went back the visitors without troubling the scorers, and Tim Thirkell led the attack beautifully with 2-25. Sam Boyns backed him up with 2-26 and a couple of miserly maidens thrown in for good measure, while Michael Beeney weighed in with 2-37 — the sort of tidy, shared bowling effort that keeps a captain's blood pressure down.

Chasing 182, David Castle played the innings of the day — a lovely 87 that carried us home — well supported by Richard Piper (34) and Tom Murphy (27). We got there at 183/4 from 33.1 overs, six wickets in hand.

Castle now sits third in this season's 1st XI run charts with 372, and Beeney fourth in the wickets with 24 — both quietly building rather good seasons while the rest of us cheer from the boundary rope.

A thoroughly good day for the club, all told — the kind that makes the teas taste that bit sweeter.

From the captain's chair — Saturday 19 September 2009.
