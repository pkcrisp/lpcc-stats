Sun 28 Sep 1997 🧢 1st XIv DS Dummy Site · HomeformDWLDD

Sunday 28th September, and the last home fixture of the '97 season saw us welcome Dummy Site, with the toss lost and the unwelcome sight of pads coming off before the tea urn had even boiled — we were put into the field.

They made hay early, closing on a stiff 152/7 from their 25 overs. Mike Elworthy top-scored with a fluent 44, well backed by Rob Doick's 29, and had it not been for Carl Davis (2-25) keeping his head while the boundaries flowed, and useful support from both Thirkells — Daniel (2-35) and Tim (1-16) — it might have got away from us altogether.

Chasing 153 is never a stroll on our ground, but the lads set about it with more composure than I had any right to expect from the pavilion steps. Daniel Thirkell, having already done his bit with the ball, then produced the innings of the day — an unbeaten, well-crafted 54 — while Roly Edwards chipped in handsomely with 37 and Nigel Thirkell added a tidy 20. We finished on 160/6, though not quite in time to snatch it outright.

Worth noting, amid all this, that Nigel continues his quietly magnificent season, now sitting second in the 1st XI's run charts with 827 to his name — a statistic rather better organised than my field placings.

In the end, honours were shared, neither side able to force the issue. A draw, then — the great English compromise, and on this evidence, a fair one.

Sun 28 Sep 1997 🧢 1st XIv DS Dummy Site · HomeformLDWLD

Sunday 28 September saw us welcome Dummy Site to Linton Park for what turned into a proper Sunday afternoon arm-wrestle, honours finishing share and share alike. Winning the toss and putting them in, we watched the visitors post a respectable 160/6 from their 25, Matt Large (37) the chief architect of that total before turning his hand to bowling duties with equal distinction. It was Stuart Cheesman who did the real damage though, 2-8 figures that had the tea urn talk turning admiring rather than anxious, with David Castle (1-19) and Large himself (1-23) chipping away at the other end.

I managed 29 of my own, more by accident than design, but 152/7 was where the innings ran out of road, eight short of the target and a draw the fairest result for two sides who'd matched each other stride for stride.

The real story of the day, though, belonged to David Castle, who brought up 15,000 career runs for the club — a landmark that rather puts my 29 in perspective, and one worth raising a glass to in the bar afterwards. With Castle leading our season's run charts on 1364 and Large topping the wickets on 49, the old firm remain in fine fettle. A draw, then, but one that felt like honest work rewarded — no complaints from this captain.
