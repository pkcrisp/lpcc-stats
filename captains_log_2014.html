Sat 27 Sep 2014🧢 1st XIv Kennington CC, Kent · HomeformWWWWW

Saturday 27th September, and a warm and showery afternoon greeted us for the visit of Kennington CC, Kent. Winning the toss, I elected to field — and for once I shall permit myself a small pat on the back, for the right call it proved to be.

Kennington posted 153 for 9 from their forty overs, built largely around Chris Drakeley's watchful 58 not out and useful support from David Finch's 25. The rest of their order found less joy against us: John Cunningham and Oliver Matthews both departed without so much as a nudge off the mark. With the ball, Martin Harris was the pick of the attack with 2 for 17, well backed up by Joshua Knowler's 2 for 18 and the ever-parsimonious Tim Thirkell, whose 1 for 15 (with two maidens thrown in for good measure) told its own tale of discipline.

Chasing 154, we strolled home at 154 for 1 in 26.4 overs, James Piper the architect with a superb 73 not out, ushered along by Richard Piper's 28 and Will Thirkell's unbeaten 27 — extras chipping in a generous 26 besides. Rather splendidly, James's innings also brought up 6,000 career runs for the club and lifted him past Jim Hall into 25th on our all-time run-scorers' list — no small feat, and toasted accordingly in the bar afterwards.

A comfortable nine-wicket victory, then, and a thoroughly pleasant way to spend a showery Saturday. Onwards we go.

Sun 21 Sep 2014🧢 Sunday 1st XIv Tenterden CC · AwayformLWWWW

Sunday 21 September found us on the road to Tenterden, under skies of that particular English grey that promises rain and never quite delivers it. I won the toss and, with the usual reckless optimism of a captain who fancies his top order, stuck us in.

Daniel Thirkell had a torrid afternoon behind the stumps, 20 byes going begging.

Elsewhere, James Piper continues to top our Sunday run charts with 259 for the season, and Martin Harris sits third among the wicket-takers with 14. But today belonged to Tenterden: beaten by seven wickets, we live to fight another Sunday.

Sat 20 Sep 2014🧢 1st XIv Northfleet CC · HomeformWWWWW

Northfleet were bowled out for 90 in 35.3 overs.

A special mention, too, for Richard Piper, who marked his 600th appearance in Linton colours — a landmark worth far more than the runs he added. Two points, won by 150 runs: emphatic, thoroughly earned, and a rare afternoon when everything I touched turned to gold.
