Sun 26 Sep 2010 🧢 Sunday 1st XIv CC Chelsfield CC · HomeformWWLWW

We won the toss on Sunday and I had no hesitation in taking first knock — a decision I'm rather pleased to say vindicated itself as the day wore on. Batting first against Chelsfield, we posted a tidy 165 for 8 from our 42 overs, the innings built around a really well-crafted fifty from Richard Piper, who made 66 and continues to bat like a man on a mission this summer. Twenty-five extras crept in too, which I like to think of as Chelsfield's small but generous contribution to the cause.

Out in the field, the lads set about defending the total with real purpose. Chelsfield were restricted to 141 for 9 from their 41, with I. Walker unbeaten on 58 the chief obstacle, well supported by M. Pearcy's 20 — though X Robinson and X Ingram went the way of all flesh without so much as a run to their names. The bowling honours belonged to Dan Barkaway, whose miserly 4 for 36, including five maidens, did the real damage, with Sam Boyns (3-10) and Jon Harris (1-28) chipping in handsomely around him.

In the end neither side could quite force it home, and honours were shared. Richard Piper now leads our 2010 Sunday 1st XI run charts with 521 for the season, and Sam Boyns sits third in the wickets with 17 — both quietly building rather memorable summers. A draw, then, but a thoroughly honourable one, and no shame at all in sharing the spoils with a side that made us work for every run.

Sun 19 Sep 2010 🧢 Sunday 1st XIv TC Tenterden CC · HomeformDWWLW

We knew coming in that Tenterden would test us, and after winning the toss I fancied a bat first on what looked a decent pitch — one of the better decisions I've made this season, if I say so myself. A 34-run win, two points banked, and Rawson's redemption arc complete within a single afternoon. With Piper still topping the Sunday run charts and Boyns lurking fourth among the wicket-takers, the season ticks along nicely. All told: a fine team performance, and — dare I say it — thoroughly professional stuff from the Duck Club.
