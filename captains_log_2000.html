Sat 30 Sep 2000 🧢 1st XIv BC Bidborough CC · HomeformDWLLD

Here is our Saturday afternoon, Bidborough CC in town, and me having lost the toss for what feels like the ninth week running. Sent in, we bowled and held our nerve as Bidborough compiled 133 all out from their 46 overs, R. Warner leading the way with 27, ably assisted by Craig Denness (21) and Ian Moore (20). John Taylor was the pick of the attack, a fine 4-22, with David Castle chipping in at 3-10 and Michael Beeney doing what Beeney does — 1-15 and six maidens of positively monastic discipline. Poor Piers behind the stumps had a torrid afternoon of it, nine byes his reward for standing where the rest of us dare not.

Chasing 134, we made harder work of it than the scoreboard perhaps suggests, closing on 136 for 5 from our 46. David Castle carried the innings again with 32, well supported by Mike Elworthy (30 not out) and Daniel Thirkell (28 not out), while your humble captain contributed a tidy 32 of his own — and, in a fit of showing off, three wickets with the ball too. Spare a thought for Stuart Cheesman, who departed for a golden duck; these things happen to the best of us, Stuart, and no doubt you'll remind me of my next failure with interest.

Four wickets the margin, two points banked, and a thoroughly deserved win. David Castle continues his fine season, now third in the club's run charts, and Beeney sits proudly atop the wicket-takers. A good day at the office, and one for the scrapbook.

🗓️ 23 Sep – 29 Sep 2000 · 2000 — 2 matches

Sun 24 Sep 2000 🧢 1st XIv AC Anchorians CC · HomeformWDWLL

Sunday 24 September saw us welcome Anchorians CC to Linton Park, and for once the coin toss actually behaved itself — heads, and into the field went our visitors while I sent us in to bat, feeling uncharacteristically decisive for a man who usually agonises over these things for a full five minutes.

What followed was as brisk a knock as this reporter can remember. Russell Baldwin, whose season has been quietly magnificent (he now sits second in the 1st XI run charts with 575 for the year), batted with real authority for his unbeaten 37, and found an excellent ally in Clive Newman, calm and correct for his 25 not out. I managed to get a bat on a few myself for 25, more through good fortune than good technique, and between us we rattled along to 65 without loss in just 15.1 overs — the sort of over rate that would have had our openers of decades past reaching for the smelling salts.

Alas, the cricketing gods decided that was quite enough entertainment for one Sunday, and neither side could force matters to a conclusion. Honours were shared, the teas were good, and Anchorians departed none the worse for wear.

A draw, then — not the stuff of legend, but with Baldwin in this sort of form and the sun still out, I'll happily take it. Onwards.
