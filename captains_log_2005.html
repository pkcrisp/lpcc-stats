Sat 24 Sep 2005 🧢 1st XIv CC Chelsfield CC · HomeformWDDLW

From the captain's chair — Saturday 24 September 2005

No luck with the coin, and Chelsfield CC took first use of a good-looking pitch. Whether it was cricketing wisdom or force of habit I couldn't say, but it left us with the ball first and, for once, that suited us nicely. They battled to 161/5 from their 45 overs, with X Vaughan playing the innings of the day, 58 well-crafted runs, and H Abbett chipping in a useful 32 alongside him. Credit to the attack: Michael Beeney led from the front with 2-37, Jon Harris and Daniel Thirkell chimed in with a wicket apiece, and Darren Styles bowled two maidens with the kind of miserliness that makes batsmen mutter into their gloves.

Chasing 162, we made surprisingly serene work of it, reaching the target for the loss of just three wickets with eleven overs unused. Richard Piper was the architect, unbeaten on 58, calm as you like, with Mark Christmas lending real substance in support with 45. Daniel Thirkell finished not out on 25, continuing what has been a golden season for him — he already tops our 2005 run charts with 857 and sits second in the wicket-takers' list with 23. I managed a modest 25 myself, more nudge than nurdle, but every run counts.

Seven wickets the margin, and not a jangled nerve in sight. A thoroughly good day for the club, and one I shall enjoy retelling long after the finer details have conveniently blurred.
