Sun 22 Sep 2013🧢 Sunday 1st XIv Tenterden CC · HomeformWWWLW

It was Tenterden CC who joined us at home on Sunday, and losing the toss meant a spell in the field before we'd so much as unpacked the flask of tea. Not that it mattered in the end — put in to bowl first, they instead found themselves on the wrong end of a proper Sunday flogging.

James Piper was simply immense, carrying his bat to an unbeaten century, with Will Thirkell chipping in a handsome 66 and Richard Knowler 22 for good measure. We did wobble to 2-1 early doors — enough to have me reaching for the Rennies — but Piper and Thirkell's 125-run stand for the fifth wicket put the game beyond doubt, poor Mark Christmas's golden duck notwithstanding. 229/6 from 41.1 overs was a total to be proud of.

In the field, Christmas made amends in style, ripping through with 3-12, well supported by Thirkell (3-16) and Michael Beeney (3-48), while Richard Knowler kept things tight with two maidens. Tenterden rallied through Ben Price (61) and James Rand (44), but three ducks and a middle-order collapse from 93-3 to 99-6 did for them, and they folded for 188. As for your skipper, a golden blob with the bat was somewhat softened by three wickets with the ball — least I could do.

A word too for Will Thirkell, who brought up 3,000 runs for the club — a wonderful landmark, thoroughly deserved. Nine byes gave poor Kevin Presneill a torrid afternoon behind the stumps, but no matter: victory by 41 runs, two points banked, and a very satisfying drive home.
