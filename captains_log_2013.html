🗓️ 21 Sep – 27 Sep 2013 · 2013 — 1 match

Sun 22 Sep 2013🧢 Sunday 1st XIv Tenterden CC · HomeformWWWLW

It was Tenterden CC who joined us at home on Sunday, and losing the toss meant a spell in the field before we'd so much as unpacked the flask of tea. Not that it mattered in the end — put in to bowl first, they instead found themselves on the wrong end of a proper Sunday flogging.

James Piper was simply immense, carrying his bat to an unbeaten century, with Will Thirkell chipping in a handsome 66 and Richard Knowler 22 for good measure. We did wobble to 2-1 early doors — enough to have me reaching for the Rennies — but Piper and Thirkell's 125-run stand for the fifth wicket put the game beyond doubt, poor Mark Christmas's golden duck notwithstanding. 229/6 from 41.1 overs was a total to be proud of.

In the field, Christmas made amends in style, ripping through with 3-12, well supported by Thirkell (3-16) and Michael Beeney (3-48), while Richard Knowler kept things tight with two maidens. Tenterden rallied through Ben Price (61) and James Rand (44), but three ducks and a middle-order collapse from 93-3 to 99-6 did for them, and they folded for 188. As for your skipper, a golden blob with the bat was somewhat softened by three wickets with the ball — least I could do.

A word too for Will Thirkell, who brought up 3,000 runs for the club — a wonderful landmark, thoroughly deserved. Nine byes gave poor Kevin Presneill a torrid afternoon behind the stumps, but no matter: victory by 41 runs, two points banked, and a very satisfying drive home.

🗓️ 14 Sep – 20 Sep 2013 · 2013 — 1 match

Wed 18 Sep 2013🧢 Midweek XIv Penshurst Park CC · AwayformLWLDL

It was fielding first for me on Wednesday 18th, when we made the trip out to Penshurst Park CC — and I'll admit I rather fancied our chances putting them in. The right call, as it turned out. Penshurst crawled to 120/8 from their forty overs, with only Sean Axtell (34 not out) offering any real resistance. Tom Fordyce and Mike Croft were sent back for ducks apiece, and the real story was with the ball: Thomas Beeney was a menace, mopping up 3-13, with James Piper (1-13) and the ever-parsimonious Matthew Carter (1-15, three maidens and not a run given away lightly) squeezing the life out of the innings around him.

Chasing 121 should never have been the formality it looked, but Richard Piper's 30 set us on our way, and Peter Whyte (26 not out) and James Piper (24 not out) saw us home in some comfort — 121/3 in 31.4 overs, seven wickets to spare. Poor Will Thew will want to forget his contribution, bagging a golden duck, but nobody remembers the blot when the job's done.

A nice personal landmark too: James Piper brought up his 50th career wicket, and with it further burnished his claim to top spot in this season's Midweek XI run charts, while Thomas Beeney's 3-13 nudges him along nicely in the wicket column. All told, a thoroughly good day at the office — professional with the ball, comfortable with the bat, and the sort of win that makes the drive home feel a good deal shorter.
