Sun 24 Sep 2017🧢 Sunday 1st XIv Tenterden CC · AwayformWWDWD

We were away at Tenterden last Sunday, 24th September, under a grey but perfectly agreeable sky. I lost the toss — a habit I'm rather perfecting this season — and we were asked to field first, in the loosest sense of the word, since it was our openers who did the actual leather-hunting later.

Batting first, we compiled a handy 191/8 from our 41 overs, built almost entirely around Tobey Castle, who batted with real class for an unbeaten 86, and found willing company in Darrell Houghton-Judge's 33 — the pair's 58-run fourth-wicket stand the platform for everything that followed. Elsewhere in the order Luke Welsman kept the scorebook honest with a golden duck, in the fine tradition of these things.

In the field, 191 always looked defendable, and so it nearly proved. Tenterden replied with 184/8 from their 44, Guy Day making a classy 61 and getting good support from Joe Betteridge (40) and Mark Kokkinos (20), their sixth-wicket pair adding a stubborn 55. But Tobey Castle, not content with his runs, took the ball and ran through them for 4-30, with Tim Thirkell miserly as ever (2-18, four maidens) and James Piper chipping in with 1-29. Nine byes did rather give the game away for Sam Boyns, bless him.

A special mention for Darrell Houghton-Judge, reaching his 100th appearance in Linton colours — a proper landmark. In the end honours were shared, a draw that flattered neither side but robbed neither of pride either. All told, an afternoon that did us credit.

Sun 17 Sep 2017🧢 Sunday 1st XIv Sidcup CC · HomeformWWWDW

Sunday 17 September, and Sidcup CC rolled into Linton Park on a damp, faintly menacing afternoon — the kind where the covers grumble but the game goes on regardless. A draw, then, and on another day we surely finish the job. Still, with bowling like that on show, I'll take it as a very useful afternoon's work.
