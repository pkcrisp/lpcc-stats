Sat 24 Sep 2016 🧢 1st XIv Stowting CC · HomeformWWWWW

Off warm fell tumble Stowting's way, and truth be told I still can't quite believe it. Winning the toss on a warm, cloudy afternoon at home, I fancied batting first, and for once the lads made their captain look clever. We were away in fine style, the openers putting on 65 before the first breakthrough, and while there was the odd wobble — poor Luke Welsman and Martin Harris both back in the hutch without troubling the scorers — the middle order simply refused to be bothered by such trifles. A magnificent 115-run stand for the fourth wicket was the innings' spine, built on the extraordinary Tobey Castle, unbeaten on 116, with James Piper (58) and Jordan Austin (33) both playing their part handsomely. 244/6 from our forty overs felt like a total with some substance to it.

Defending it was another matter, and Stowting made us work every run of the way, closing on 209/7 with David Jackson (62) and useful contributions from Mike Christie (37) and Troy Harding (35) keeping them in the hunt, aided by a stubborn 72-run fourth-wicket stand. But James Piper, having already done his bit with the bat, turned his arm over to devastating effect with 2-7, well backed by Tim Thirkell and Jacob Ryan, the latter's two maidens proving there's no such thing as a cheap over against this lot. Fifteen byes did rather suggest poor Nat Presneill was having one of those afternoons behind the stumps, but no matter.

A 35-run win and two points banked — a proper team effort, and one I'll happily take credit for from the captain's chair.

Sun 18 Sep 2016 🧢 Sunday 1st XIv TC Tenterden CC · HomeformLWLWL

It rained runs at Linton Park on Sunday, and for once the man doing the raining wasn't the sky.

Winning the toss on a warm, cloudy afternoon, I did what any captain with half an eye on the tea urn does and stuck us in. 198 for 9 wasn't enough. Thirty-four runs the margin, two points the reward, and thoroughly deserved both.
