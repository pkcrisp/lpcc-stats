Wed 29 Sep 1982 🧢 Midweek XIv DS Dummy Site · AwayformLDDDL

Wednesday 29 September, and the road took us to Dummy Site with autumn very much in the air and, I'm pleased to say, not a hint of rain to trouble the covers. I won the toss and had no hesitation in taking first knock — the right call, I felt, and I'll stand by it. Paul Bowles played the innings of the day, 57 crafted with the sort of assurance that reminds you why he tops our run charts, while Ian Hammond's 32 and Dave Latter's 21 gave the total real backbone. 176/7 from our 45 overs looked, at tea, like a total worth defending.

And defend it we very nearly did. Darby gave the opposition hope with a fine 59, Ray Grayland chipping in 25 and Jenner 20, but Mike Elworthy simply refused to be tamed — 5 for 31, five maidens among them, bowling with the relentlessness of a man determined to add to his season's haul. Jon Harris (2-54) and Steve Gower (1-15) did their bit too, and Walters and Bailey trudged back for ducks without so much as troubling the scorers. Poor Jim Hall had a torrid afternoon behind the stumps, 14 byes leaking away like the season itself.

In the end it was four runs that did for us — the width of a misfield, the shadow of a missed single. We'll be better for it next week.

Sun 26 Sep 1982 🧢 1st XIv Linton Park CC · HomeformLLDWD

Sunday 26 September brought us out on our travels, and for once the toss actually mattered — I called it right and had no hesitation in taking first knock, which I still maintain was the correct decision, whatever the scorebook says by the end.

The lads set about building something substantial, and 200 all out in 25 overs is not to be sneezed at. Andy Woollett anchored things nicely with an unbeaten 21, and he had fine company in Sean Riley and Richard Ashby, both finishing not out on 20 apiece — a touch of order amid what I suspect was some fairly agricultural hitting further down. The extras column weighed in with a generous 28, which either speaks well of our running between the wickets or rather less well of the opposition's line. I contributed a modest 20 myself, more workmanlike than watchable.

The real story of the day, though, belonged to Mike Iliffe, who brought up 3,000 career runs for the club — a landmark achieved with the sort of quiet application that deserves rather more fanfare than a cricket tea can provide. Elsewhere, Nigel Thirkell continues to sit second in this year's 1st XI run charts with 1,141 to his name, while young Simon Thirkell leads the 2nd XI standings, albeit modestly with 21.

As for the match itself, 200 proved not nearly enough, and we were beaten by seven wickets. Heads up, chins up, and back to the nets — there's cricket yet to be won this season.

Sat 18 Sep 1982 🧢 1st XIv West Farleigh CC · HomeformWLLLD

Saturday 18 September 1982. Won the toss, and — perhaps unfashionably these days — I fancied batting first against West Farleigh. The lads didn't let me down, 211 for 3 off our 46 overs, with Nigel Thirkell playing an absolute blinder for an unbeaten 111. Paul Bowles chipped in handsomely with 56, Andy Woollett saw us home unbeaten on 28, and poor Brian Piper made his contribution with the bat count for precisely nothing — the sort of duck that gets you a pint bought in sympathy and a nickname you never quite shake off.

West Farleigh could only manage 100 for 8 from their 44 overs, with Fred Baker's 24 the only real resistance and Norman Underdown departing without troubling the scorers. Brian, evidently deciding batting wasn't his afternoon's calling, made amends spectacularly with the ball — 2 for 7, best figures of the day. Paul Bowles and Mike Elworthy weren't far behind with two apiece, Mike bowling with the miserliness of a man checking his own change, five maidens to his name. I managed 56 myself, which I mention purely for the record and not at all for the pleasure of it.

A comfortable 111-run victory, then, and a timely reminder — with Paul still topping our run charts and Mike third among the wicket-takers this season — that Linton Park in 1982 are rather good fun to watch. A thoroughly good day for the club.
