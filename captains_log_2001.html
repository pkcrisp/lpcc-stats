Sun 23 Sep 2001 🧢 1st XIv AC Anchorians CC · HomeformDWDLW

Sunday last brought the Anchorians to Linton Park, and having won the toss I did what any self-respecting skipper with a suspicious sky and an even more suspicious top order does — I stuck them in. For a while it looked the sort of decision they write limericks about, as M. Vowels compiled a serene 75 not out, with Gary Myers (41) and Eddie Stanford (35) offering cheerful assistance, and 200/3 off 29 overs went up on the board rather more briskly than any of us would have liked. Only Darren Styles emerged with real credit, snaffling 2-70 and, more tellingly, sending down two maidens — the sort of miserly spell that has opposition batsmen muttering into their gloves.

Chasing 201, we might have feared the worst, but Russell Baldwin had other ideas. He and Clive Newman simply refused to be parted, Baldwin purring to 75 not out and Newman a composed 30 not out, the pair carrying us to 108 without loss from 17.2 overs before the innings — and the afternoon — ran out of road. A draw it was, honours shared, and nobody terribly upset about it.

Worth noting, as the season charts remind us, that Baldwin now sits second in the club's run-scoring列 for 2001 with 1053 to his name, and Styles fourth among the wicket-takers with 22. Not a bad afternoon's work, then. A draw against a side that put 200 on the board is no disgrace — call it a point salvaged, pride intact, and the tea urn, as ever, the real match-winner.

Sat 22 Sep 2001 🧢 2nd XIv EC Elliotts CC · HomeformWDWLW

Saturday 22 September 2001 saw us welcome Elliotts CC, and losing the toss meant we were straight into the field — never my favourite way to spend a Saturday afternoon, but there we were.

Elliotts settled in nicely and posted 185/7 from their 48 overs, built around a classy half-century from Ian Crickmore (59), with Steve Swindell (35) and Dave Gracey (22) chipping in to keep the scoreboard ticking. Credit where it's due, though: John Taylor was magnificent with the ball, 4 for 54 and five maidens besides, bowling like the pitch owed him money. Dave Todd (2-53) and Trevor Back (1-20) did their bit too, and between them we kept Elliotts to something gettable.

Chasing 186 from 41 overs, we made a fair fist of it. Russell Baldwin played the innings of the day, 68 sailing well past his fifty, with sterling support from D. Bunch (38) and John Taylor — clearly not content with his bowling exploits — unbeaten on 20. Poor Richard Ellis, though, will want this one struck from the record: out for a duck, and no doubt already plotting his revenge.

In the end we fell short at 174/9, honours shared in a draw that satisfied precisely nobody and disappointed nobody either. A fair result, all told — Elliotts had the better of the toss and the bowling figures were ours. Trevor Back continues to top our 2001 run charts and John Taylor sits third among the wicket-takers, so the season's shaping up nicely even if Saturday's verdict was a stalemate.

Sat 22 Sep 2001 🧢 1st XIv DC Detling CC · AwayformWDWDL

As I strode out for the toss on the 22nd, I confess a little swagger crept into my step, and correctly calling it sealed the deal — no hesitation from me, we were batting.

David Castle set about proceedings with the sort of assured, unhurried authority that makes a captain's job look easy, ambling to 84 not out and, along the way, quietly bringing up 19,000 career runs for the club — a landmark that rather deserves a pint or two in its honour. He wasn't short of company either, with Adrian Caddick (33 not out) and Piers McDonald (28) chipping in handsomely as we closed on a healthy 178/4 from our 42 overs.

Defending that total, Detling never really got going. Barry Town's 32 was the only real resistance on offer, while poor Andrew Archer and Andy O'Nions departed without so much as a nibble at the scorers' pencil. The damage was done chiefly by Mike Redman, whose figures of 3-3, including three miserly maidens, bordered on the surgical, with Michael Beeney (2-9) and Howard Waters (2-17) mopping up the rest. Detling were bowled out for 95 in 34.4 overs, and the game was ours by 83 runs.

A pleasing personal contribution, certainly, but the day belonged to the collective effort — and to David Castle's milestone, which puts the cherry firmly atop the cake. Two points, thoroughly earned, and a fine advert for why we turn up every Saturday.

And yet, only Linton Park could contrive to be beaten by seven wickets after all that. Cricket has a way of humbling you just when you think you've cracked it. We'll be better for it next week.
