Sun 27 Sep 1998 🧢 1st XIv Linton Park CC · HomeformWDLWW

Wound the toss, called it right, and never once doubted the decision to bat first on a fine Sunday at the end of September – 27th, if the diary's to be believed. What followed was 30 overs of proper, old-fashioned batting: Paul Gibson playing an innings of real substance for 78, Michael Beeney matching him stroke for stroke on the way to 56, and Clive Newman chipping in a tidy 45 alongside them. We finished on 235 for 7, which on any club pitch looks like a winning total, and for a good while it felt like one. Only poor Peter Whyte will want to forget the day in a hurry, done for a duck before he'd had time to take guard properly – these things happen to the best of us, and doubtless will again.

There was a moment worth savouring regardless of the result: Paul Gibson brought up 11,000 career runs for the club, a landmark that ought to be toasted properly in the bar rather than buried in a scorecard. Elsewhere in the season's ledger, Clive Newman continues his fine form, sitting second in the 1st XI run charts with 610, while Roly Edwards leads the Sunday 2nd XI standings with 70.

But 235 wasn't enough. We fell short by 26 runs, our bowlers unable to defend what looked, at tea, like a match-winning score. Not our day, then – heads up, chins out, and back to the nets for the lot of us.

Sat 26 Sep 1998 🧢 1st XIv BC Bidborough CC · HomeformWWDLW

Saturday 26th September saw Bidborough CC visit Linton Park, and winning the toss I had no hesitation in taking first knock — a decision I'm rather pleased to say vindicated itself.

We were bowled out for 167 in 36.5 overs, a total built on Clive Newman's fine 42, with Brian Piper (37) and David Castle (21) chipping in nicely. Less said the better about Nigel Thirkell and Jon Harris, both departing for ducks, though every side needs its sacrificial lambs.

But the day belonged to Matt Large, whose 8 for 44 was a spell for the ages — a five-for and thoroughly deserved. Daniel Thirkell and John Taylor lent honest support with a wicket apiece, and Nigel Thirkell bowled three tight maidens to choke the runs. As for your captain, 16 with the bat and, happily, 8 wickets with the ball — not a bad afternoon's work, even if I say so myself.

With Piers McDonald still leading the season's run charts on 673 and Matt Large now second in the wickets列 on 26, the campaign continues in fine health. We got home by 7 runs — tight, tense, and thoroughly deserved. A good day for the club, and a very good day for the skipper's ego.

Sun 20 Sep 1998 🧢 1st XIv AC Anchorians CC · HomeformLWWDL

Nigel Thirkell 105 not out, Piers McDonald 72 — a fine Sunday's work for the ledger.

Sunday last, Anchorians came to Linton Park, and having called correctly at the toss I had no hesitation in taking first knock — one of those rare captaincy decisions that actually looked clever by teatime. Nigel Thirkell played the innings of the season, unbeaten on a majestic 105, while Piers McDonald wasn't far behind with a stylish 72. Between them they carried us to a handsome 220 for 5 off our 35 overs. Elsewhere in the order things were rather less romantic — Richard Piper and Trevor Back both fell for ducks, and the pair of them can expect to hear about it in the bar for some weeks yet.

With 220 on the board, defending it was always going to be the easier half of the job, and so it proved. Stuart Cheesman set the tone with 2 for 15, well backed up by Jon Harris (2 for 19) and Daniel Thirkell (1 for 1), while Trevor Back made amends for his batting with two tidy maidens. Anchorians were bowled out for 115 in 31 overs — though I must confess 11 byes crept into the total, and poor Adam Pooley behind the stumps had rather a testing afternoon of it.

A personal 105 not out was, I'll admit, thoroughly satisfying — though the real story was a total team performance, bat and ball alike. A fine win by 105 runs, and with Piers McDonald and Jon Harris both flying at the top of this season's charts, the 1st XI ship looks to be steering a very true course. Onwards.
