Sun 02 Oct 1977 🧢 Marriedv Linton Park CC · HomeformLDDDL

Sunday 2 October brought us to enemy territory as visitors, and for once the coin obliged — I called correctly and never seriously considered fielding first. Batting looked the sensible road to a result, and so it proved, up to a point.

Paul Bowles played the innings of the day, striding to 74 with the kind of assurance that makes a captain feel unreasonably clever for winning a toss. Tim Thirkell chipped in handsomely with 52, and between them they hauled us to a respectable 161 for 9 from our 41 overs. Less happily, Colin Dicker, Martin Mears and Keith Miller all trooped back for ducks — a trio of blobs that rather took the shine off an otherwise cheerful scorecard, and no doubt the source of some ribbing in the clubhouse this week.

Elsewhere in the season's ledger, Jim Hall continues his steady climb up the 1st XI run charts, now 5th with 316 to his name, Paul Bowles heads the Married averages on the strength of today's 74, and Brian Woodcock leads the Singles with a tidy 50.

As for the match itself, our bowlers and fielders were given too much to defend and too little margin for error, and we were duly seen off by 9 wickets. Not our finest afternoon, but no cause for gloom — the runs are there, the form is coming, and we'll be considerably better for it next week.

Sat 01 Oct 1977 🧢 1st XIv Lordswood CC · HomeformWWDLD

A Saturday in October, and the toss went against us as tosses so often do when there's a hint of chill in the air — Lordswood CC elected to bat, and we settled for first use of the ball instead of the willow. They ground their way to 130 for 6 from their 53 overs, Jon Harris top-scoring with 25, then chipping in again with 22, and Keith Miller weighing in with 20 of his own. With the ball, though, we were tidy enough to keep the visitors honest: Jon Harris led the line with 3 for 42 and seven maidens to his name — not a run given away lightly — while Keith Miller (2 for 18) and John Bloomfield (1 for 25) mopped up either end. I even found a bit of rhythm myself, picking up three wickets, which for a captain of my vintage counts as a minor miracle.

Chasing 130 proved rather trickier than it looked on paper, and we limped to 95 for 6 from our 40 overs. Paul Bowles was the shining light, unbeaten on 68 with a thoroughly well-made fifty, carrying the innings almost single-handedly while Ian Webb, Tim Thirkell and Brian Woodcock each contributed a golden duck to the cause 🦆.

Up the season's ledger Paul Bowles now leads our 1977 run charts with 1,581, and Jon Harris tops the wickets with 83 — the two pillars of our summer once again holding firm. In the end, honours were shared: a draw when neither side could quite force the issue, and on this evidence, a fair result.

Wed 28 Sep 1977 🧢 Midweek XIv The Mote CC · AwayformWLDLD

Wednesday 28th September, and off we trundled to The Mote, losing the toss and asked to field first – hardly the tonic for a September afternoon, but the lads set about it with good cheer.

The Mote were bowled out for 113 in 42.4 overs, and the innings had our fingerprints all over it. J. Shepherd top-scored with 22, David Twiddy chipping in 21, but at the other end it was carnage: Eddie Page and David Sayer both walked back without troubling the scorers. Allan Cooper was the chief tormentor, spinning his way to a magnificent 5-39 with eight miserly maidens thrown in for good measure – not a man to get careless against on a slow one. Trevor Back (3-44) and Jon Harris (2-25) mopped up admirably around him.

Chasing 114, we made harder work of it than the arithmetic suggested we might, but got home on 117/4 from 38 overs. Nigel Thirkell led the way with a composed 34, well supported by Paul Bowles's 33, and between them they saw us safely across the line.

A word for the record books: Allan Cooper's five-for sees him leapfrog Frank Taylor into 15th on the club's all-time wicket-takers list – onwards and upwards. Thirkell, meanwhile, continues to top the Midweek XI run charts with 230 for the season, while Harris leads the wickets with 21.

A fine win by six wickets, then, and a satisfying one to bank before the nights draw in. Onwards.
