Sun 23 Sep 2007 🧢 Sunday 1st XIv ? · HomeformDDWDL

The lads knock up 234, we let a draw slip through our grasp — perfect fodder for a gazette report.

Sunday 23rd, the coin obliged, and I had no hesitation putting us in — a decision that, for once, looked rather clever by tea. Richard Piper set about the bowling with real intent for 91, and when he finally departed we found ourselves grateful for a seventh-wicket stand worth exactly 100 with Jim Hall, who finished unbeaten on a composed 53. Nigel Thirkell chipped in a handy 26 along the way, and 234 for 7 from our 45 overs felt like a total with a bit of bite to it.

And bite it did, for a while. Nine down and nowhere to go, they somehow clung on at 140 for 9. Honours shared, then — a draw that felt, in truth, like two points we'd rather have banked, but with Piper and Newman both riding high in the season's charts, there's no shortage of reasons to keep smiling.

Sat 22 Sep 2007 🧢 1st XIv SC Sidcup CC · HomeformLLWLW

It was Sidcup CC at home on Saturday, and I lost the toss again — a run now stretching long enough that I suspect the coin itself has switched allegiance. They chose to bat, and by tea we'd have happily swapped places. Tim Dawes played beautifully for 89, Paul Jenkins carved out an unbeaten 56, and Daniel Corbett chipped in with 47, the pair of them putting on 89 for the fourth wicket after we'd had them 1 down early and dared to dream. Credit where due: Mark Christmas bowled with real discipline, two maidens among his 1-25, with Tim Wright and Jack Jenner both weighing in with a wicket apiece. Mark Amphlett departed for a duck at our hands, small mercies. Sidcup closed on 219/5.

Chasing 220, we never quite got the legs under us. Jack Jenner, Tim Thirkell and Carl Davis all made the long walk back for nought, which is never a pleasant trio to keep company with in the book. All out 179 in 43.5 overs.

Beaten by 40 runs, then, and fairly so — Sidcup's middle order simply had more in the tank than ours. Craig Sands continues to sit proudly second in the season's run charts and Mark Christmas top of the wickets, so the engine room is in good order even when the rest of us stall. We'll regroup, we'll bowl straighter, and we'll be better for it next week.

A tense, thoroughly deserved win by two wickets. Onwards.
