Sun 26 Sep 2004 🧢 1st XIv DS Dummy Site · HomeformWWLWD

Sunday last saw us welcome Dummy Site, and it began as these things so often do for us: I lost the toss, they fancied batting, and I trudged back to the boundary before a ball had been bowled. Twenty overs later they'd posted a brisk 130/6, which felt about par until Gareth Davies decided otherwise, mixing it up beautifully for 2-16 and setting a tone that Dennis Salisbury (2-17) and Daniel Thirkell (1-20) were only too happy to follow. A proper team effort with the ball, and we came off feeling rather pleased with ourselves.

Chasing 131, the top order rather let the middle order do the heavy lifting, and thank heavens they did. Mike Elworthy anchored things nicely with an unbeaten 27, with Adam Jackson (26 not out) and Simon Thirkell (25 not out) both looking entirely unbothered by the situation. I wish I could say the same for myself — I fell for a duck, and the less said about that the better. Russell Baldwin joined me in the Duck Club too, so at least I had company in the walk of shame.

Amid the wreckage, some genuine cause for celebration: Tim Thirkell brought up 100 career wickets, and Dennis Salisbury reached the milestone of 50 — both thoroughly deserved and worth raising a glass to, whatever the scoreboard said. Daniel Thirkell, meanwhile, continues to lead our season run charts with 459 and sits second in the wickets list with 20.

In the end we fell five wickets short. Not our day, but with bowling and milestones like that, hardly a black one either. Back to the nets, heads up.

Sun 26 Sep 2004 🧢 1st XIv DS Dummy Site · HomeformWWWLW

Skipper's Log, Sunday 26th September 2004. Dummy Site were our visitors for what turned out to be one of those honest, hard-fought draws that leaves everybody slightly the poorer for the lack of a result, and slightly the richer for having had a proper game of cricket.

Winning the toss, I had no hesitation in taking first knock — and for once my judgement wasn't laughed out of the pavilion. We set about compiling 130/6 from our twenty overs, with Matthew Carter batting with real composure for an unbeaten 35, and Richard Knowler (26 not out) and Matt Large (26 not out) both chipping in handsomely at the other end. I even managed to contribute a watchful 26 myself, though I'll leave the lads to judge whether that was captaincy or comedy.

In the field we gave a good account of ourselves. Craig Sands, who sits a rather lofty second in this season's run charts with 430 to his name, was sent back for a duck without so much as a whisper — proof that even the in-form can have an off day. Richard Knowler was magnificent with the ball, ripping through with 3-4, well supported by Jack Jenner and Matthew Carter, who both picked up a wicket apiece for 21. Poor David Castle had a torrid afternoon behind the stumps, leaking 8 byes, but nobody's perfect on a Sunday.

The visitors clawed their way to 133/5 and pinched it by the narrowest of margins on paper, but with honours effectively shared, I'll call this an honourable, well-fought draw — the sort that keeps everyone talking in the bar long after the covers go on.

Verdict: a defeat, no dressing it up, but one bookended by a milestone worth celebrating and a top order in good order. Back to the nets, heads up — the runs are there, we just need the rest to follow suit.
