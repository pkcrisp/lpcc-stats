Sat 25 Sep 1971 🧢 1st XIv ? · HomeformWWDDW

Saturday last, and Linton Park welcomed the opposition on a day that promised much and, for once, largely delivered - at least while we had the ball in hand. Put in to field, the lads did us proud, bowling the visitors out for 135 in 39.5 overs. M Waghorn and A Pooley put on a decent stand of 31 and 20 respectively, but it was Paul Bowles who stole the show entirely, running through the order with 6 for 36 - four maidens included, which tells you nothing was coming easily off him. And yet, for all that, we contrived to lose by 7 wickets - quite how remains a mystery best pondered over a pint. Paul Bowles's remarkable season continues regardless, now top of both the run and wicket charts for 1971. As for the rest of us: heads up, and back to the nets.

Sun 19 Sep 1971 🧢 1st XIv HC Harrietsham CC · HomeformLWWDD

Sunday 19 September 1971, and Harrietsham CC came calling. I won the toss — no hesitation, we were batting, thank you very much — and the lads set about building something substantial. Cliff Anscombe led the way with a fine 39, John Thirkell chipped in with 37, and Paul Bowles added a brisk 29, ferrying us to 176/7 from our 51 overs. Not everyone enjoyed the outing, mind — poor Benny Bell endured the walk of shame for a duck, and I shan't let him forget it. I managed 37 myself, more through good fortune than good technique.

Defending it, though, was where the real fun began. Harrietsham's innings resembled a house of cards in a stiff breeze — 49 all out in just 20.5 overs, John Smith's battling 21 the only real resistance. Dennis Stones, Micky Tong and Keith Chaney all trooped back without troubling the scorers. Paul Bowles was in irresistible form, spinning his way to 5-28, while Allan Cooper strangled the life out of them with 4-7 and five miserly maidens, Jon Harris rounding things off with 1-11.

We ran out winners by 127 runs — a proper thumping. Up the season charts, Andrew Moss (563 runs) and Paul Bowles (50 wickets) continue to lead the way for the 1st XI. A thoroughly good day for the club, and one Harrietsham will not be in a hurry to repeat.

Sat 18 Sep 1971 🧢 1st XIv West Farleigh CC · HomeformLLWWD

Skipper's Log, Saturday 18 September 1971. West Farleigh CC were our visitors, and having lost the toss I was resigned to watching before batting — not, as it turned out, my afternoon of choice, but cricket rarely consults the captain's preferences.

Put into the field, we watched their openers settle in with a stand of 66 before the first wicket fell, and from there R Woolley led the way with a well-made 48, Colin Clark chipping in with 36 and Peter Cooper unbeaten on 35 as they closed on 201 for 6 from their 45 overs. Paul Bowles was the pick of our attack by some distance, his 4 for 62 doing the damage, with Nigel Thirkell weighing in at 2 for 42 and Ron Hickman fairly parsimonious with three maidens to his name. Bowles's four wickets, incidentally, nudge him past Frank Taylor into 10th on the club's all-time list — quietly historic stuff amid the toil.

Chasing 202, we had our wobbles — 19 for 1 became 38 for 4 in a rush that had me reaching for something stronger than tea — but Tim Thirkell's unbeaten 47, with Mike Iliffe's 28 and Andrew Moss's 21 (enough to see Moss past Frank Taylor into 17th on the run-scorers' list), plus a defiant fiftyish stand for the sixth wicket and a tail that wagged for a final 48, dragged us to 136 for 6 from 46 overs. My own 28 felt almost incidental. Twelve byes went begging past our keeper — a costly afternoon with the gloves.

A draw, then — honours shared, and probably a fair reflection of a match neither side quite had the wit to win.

Saturday 11th September saw Charles Arkcoll visit for what turned into a thoroughly satisfying afternoon's work. We lost the toss, were asked to field, and for once that felt like the right end of the coin — first use of the ball on our own square is not something to be sniffed at.

A word for Allan Cooper, who reached 50 career wickets in the process — a fine landmark, modestly earned. With Andrew Moss still leading our run charts on 505 and Cooper now top of the wicket pile with 43, the season has no shortage of heroes. A comfortable, well-earned win, and not before time — the tea urn has rarely tasted sweeter.
