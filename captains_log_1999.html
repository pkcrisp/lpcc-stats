Wed 22 Sep 1999 🧢 Midweek XIv Penshurst Park CC · AwayformDDWDL

I called it right and, for once, took the harder road — bat first on a Wednesday, September the 22nd, out at Penshurst Park. Whether that was captaincy or bloody-mindedness, the lads didn't argue.

We ground our way to 166 for 6 off 43 overs, which felt like a proper score without ever feeling like a big one. Mike Elworthy was immense — 89 of the finest, carrying the innings almost single-handedly and doing his level best to make it three figures before falling agonisingly short. Stuart Cheesman chipped in nicely with 39, the sort of unfussy support act every long innings needs. Elsewhere the Duck Club took its usual toll: Peter Whyte and Trevor Back both trudged off for nought, joining the season's growing and increasingly infamous roll of honour.

With the ball, we set about defending it — and Penshurst simply couldn't be shifted, closing us out unbeaten. A stalemate at the death, honours even, nobody quite able to land the killer blow.

Up on the season's run charts, Elworthy continues to make the rest of us look ordinary, his 89 pushing him to 310 for the summer and keeping him well clear at the top of the Midweek XI.

The verdict, then: a draw that flattered no one and disappointed no one — a fair result on a day when 166 always looked defendable, and very nearly wasn't enough to matter either way.

Sun 19 Sep 1999 🧢 1st XIv AC Anchorians CC · HomeformLDWLL

Anchorians CC came calling on Sunday, and having won the toss there was really only one decision to make on a day like that — we batted.

And what a time we had of it. Piers McDonald played beautifully for his 64, Daniel Thirkell wasn't far behind with a fine 58, and Clive Newman chipped in a tidy 53 of his own — three half-centuries stacking up nicely as we rattled to 261/7 from our thirty overs. Poor Richard Piper, though, will not be troubling the scorers this week beyond a golden duck; these things happen to the best of us, and I dare say the tea urn got the warmer reception of the two.

Two hundred and sixty-odd is always a total to bowl at rather than defend, and so it proved. Gary Myers led their reply with 45, well backed by Eddie Stanford's 37 and T Appleby's 31, but Anchorians never quite found the boundaries to match ours. K- Holmes was superb, snaring 3-36, with David Castle (2-25) and Stuart Cheesman (1-4) tidying up nicely around him. They closed on 201/9 — a battling effort, but a losing one.

Sixty runs the margin, two points the reward, and not a shred of luck required. David Castle continues to top our run charts this season with 943, and Jon Harris sits second in the wickets with 29 — the engine room of a very fine campaign. A thoroughly professional afternoon's work, and one the tea ladies will talk about for weeks.

Sat 18 Sep 1999 🧢 2nd XIv EC Elliotts CC · HomeformDLLLL

From the captain's chair — Saturday 18 September 1999. We hosted Elliotts CC in what I'd hoped might be a season highlight, though the coin had other ideas, coming down against us and sending us into the field first.

If losing the toss stung, the bowling at least gave us something to cheer. Dave Todd was the pick of the attack with 2-38, while the Thirkell brothers did their bit — Daniel chipping in with 1-27 and two tidy maidens, Tim adding 1-30. Beyond that, though, support was thin, and we folded for 174 in 34 overs — 53 runs shy. Special mention, if that's the word, to Jon Harris, who walked for a golden duck and can add his name to the club's ledger of shame.

Elsewhere, the season marches on regardless: Mike Elworthy sits proudly atop the 2nd XI run charts with 442, and Dave Todd leads the wickets with 36 — small consolations on an afternoon that got away from us. Not our day, then. Heads up, back to the nets, and on to the next one.

Not our day, then — Clive Newman and Michael Beeney continue to fly the flag in the season's charts, but the rest of us have some catching up to do. Heads up, chins out, and back to the nets.
