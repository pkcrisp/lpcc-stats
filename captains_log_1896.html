Mon 03 Aug 1896 🧢 1st XIv RC Rochester CC · HomeformDDLLL

Monday the 3rd of August, and Rochester CC rolled up at Linton Park with the sun doing its level best to cooperate. I lost the toss — no great shock, my coin-calling remains a standing joke among the lads — and we were asked to bowl first, which suited us rather nicely.

Rochester never quite escaped our clutches. L Cooper played the innings of the day, 80 well-made runs, with R A Arnold not far behind on a fine 73 and L A Goldie chipping in with 29. Poor E L Baker will not thank me for mentioning his duck 🦆, but mention it I must. With the ball it was a proper team effort: J N Tonge did the real damage with 3-0, well supported by K McAlpine and George Champion, both also finishing with 3-0, and I managed to find a bit of rhythm myself for three wickets.

Then came the small matter of getting the runs ourselves. Arthur Cornwallis played an innings to dine out on for years, 116 of pure class, with J N Tonge adding a brisk 76 in support. We got home by three wickets — snug, but thoroughly earned.

Milestones aplenty today: Cornwallis's century vaults him past F S W Cornwallis to the top of our all-time run charts, while Champion's three wickets lift him past W H Fryer into third on the all-time wicket-takers' list. He also leads this season's 1st XI for both runs, with 314, and wickets, with 23 — one begins to suspect the man doesn't sleep. Two points banked, and a thoroughly good day for Linton Park.
