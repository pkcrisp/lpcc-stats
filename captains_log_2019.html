Sun 22 Sep 2019🧢 Sunday 1st XIv Tenterden CC · AwayformLTLWL

Sunday 22 September, and off we trotted to Tenterden CC under skies that couldn't make up their mind — warm when the sun showed, decidedly damp when it didn't. Winning the toss and putting ourselves in the field, we watched Martin Duffield play the innings of the afternoon, a magnificent 102, with James Allen keeping him fine company on 77. They'd lost an early wicket for just 2 — a nervy start for them, though you wouldn't have known it once that second-wicket pair got stuck in and added 172 without so much as a backward glance. Poor Guy Day departed without troubling the scorers, a proper duck to remember. Luke Welsman led our attack manfully (1-24), with Anthony Karassellos miserly through his overs (1-32, two maidens) and James Piper chipping in (1-35). They closed on 252/4 from their 47 — a stiff ask.

We replied in kind, losing an early wicket for 0 of our own before Alex Keeler set about repairing things with a fine 75, well supported by James Piper's 53 and Archie Fairfax-Ross's 27; I rather enjoyed leading from the front with 53 myself. Mark Christmas endured a day best forgotten, both with a golden duck and 17 leg-byes conceded behind the stumps, though I shan't dwell on it. We closed on 250/8 from 41 — agonisingly, honours even.

A draw, then — neither side quite able to land the killer blow. Frankly, after batting like that, I'll happily settle for honours shared.

Sat 21 Sep 2019🧢 1st XIv Linden Park CC · HomeformWLLLL

Warm, grey skies at Linton Park, but nothing dampened the mood after I won the toss and chose to bat — a decision I'm rather pleased to take credit for.
