Sun 25 Sep 1960 🧢 1st XIv Linton Park CC · HomeformWLDLW

Sunday 25 September 1960 saw us on our travels as visitors, and winning the toss I did the only sensible thing a captain can do on a fine afternoon — elected to bat. It felt like the right call at the time, and I'm sticking to that story regardless of what followed.

What followed, in truth, was a batting performance best described as brief. We were bowled out with barely a murmur, and poor Len Conway contributed nought to the cause, departing for a golden duck that I'm sure he'll be hearing about in the clubhouse for weeks to come. Still, cricket has a way of offering small consolations amid the wreckage, and Pat Hardwick provided one — his 24 runs were enough to see him leapfrog Len Conway into 19th on the club's all-time run-scorers list, a modest personal triumph plucked from a collective misfortune.

Elsewhere the season rolls on regardless of our afternoon's troubles: John Thirkell continues to lead the 1st XI run charts with 672 for the summer, while Gordon Fenner tops the 2nd XI's with 11. Proof, if any were needed, that the club's story is bigger than one bad batting display.

We were beaten by 6 wickets in the end, no great surprise given the total we'd set them to chase. A humbling one, all told, but we'll dust ourselves off and be better for it next week.

Sat 17 Sep 1960 🧢 1st XIv Bearsted CC · HomeformWLWWL

There was much to celebrate off the scorecard too — Basil Sears' 200th appearance in Linton colours, Frank Brattle's 100th, and Robert Wood climbing to 20th on the club's all-time wicket-takers list. A draw, then: honours shared, pride mostly intact, and tea, as ever, the true winner of the day.
