🗓️ 21 Sep – 27 Sep 1974 · 1974 — 1 match

Sun 22 Sep 1974 🧢 1st XIv AC Anchorians CC · HomeformLLDDD

Here's the report for the gazette.

There are moments in a captain's life when you toss the coin skyward with hope in your heart, and moments when it lands the wrong way up and you're trudging out to the field regardless. Sunday was very much the latter, as Anchorians won the call and elected to bat first, leaving us to make the best of things under a September sky.

And make the best of things we did. Tony Gauna was the pick of the attack with a tidy 2-23, well backed up by Ron Hickman's 2-48 and Nigel Thirkell chipping in with 1-9, while Allan Cooper bowled with the sort of miserly discipline that suggests he simply doesn't believe in giving runs away — five maidens told their own story. Anchorians were held to 124/8 from their 44 overs, J Ward's 36 the top score, with R Durbridge (35) and P Croker (20) offering support.

Chasing it down, Cliff Anscombe played the innings of the day, 48 well-crafted runs that not only steadied our reply but nudged him past Robert Murray into 8th on the club's all-time run-scorers list — no small thing to achieve on a Sunday afternoon. Nigel Thirkell, not content with his bowling exertions, then finished the job unbeaten on 40 as we cantered home at 128/3 with overs to spare. Tony Gauna's afternoon of toil was rewarded too, his two wickets lifting him past Michael Morton into 17th among the club's wicket-takers.

Seven wickets the margin, and a thoroughly good day for the club.

Sun 15 Sep 1974 🧢 1st XIv DS Dummy Site · HomeformWLLDD

Sunday 15 September 1974, and Dummy Site were our visitors for what turned into one of those honourably indecisive afternoons club cricket does so well. I lost the toss — no shock there, my coin-calling remains a weakness rivalled only by my off-side driving — and we were put into the field.

They ground their way to 128 for 8 off 39 overs, built chiefly around A. Gosling's unbeaten 45 and useful support from F-H. Ames with 37. Nigel Thirkell was magnificent with the ball, 3 for 32 including five maidens that must have driven their middle order to distraction, while Paul Bowles chipped in with 3 for 44 and Terry Donovan took 2 for 4. I. Cloke and D. Bromley both departed without troubling the scorers.

With 128 to chase, we settled for 60 for 2 off our 23 overs, Paul Bowles top-scoring with 23 and Cliff Anscombe unbeaten on 21 — no great urgency in it, but no alarms either.

Two milestones worth raising a glass to: Cliff Anscombe brought up his 200th appearance in Linton colours, a proper landmark of loyal service, and Terry Donovan's brace nudged him past Dick Langridge into 8th on the club's all-time wicket-taking list. Meanwhile Paul Brattle continues to lead our 1974 run charts on 612, with Nigel Thirkell now second among the season's wicket-takers on 66.

A draw, then — honours shared, and honestly, about right.

Sat 14 Sep 1974 🧢 1st XIv SC Southborough CC · AwayformDWLLD

Turned the corner into Southborough on a fine September Saturday, won the toss, and – with the sky looking amenable – had few second thoughts about batting first. Gordon Dent played the innings of the day, a well-crafted 68 that gave the rest of us licence to play around him, Jon Harris chipping in with a tidy 39 and Paul Brattle 21 as we posted 175/6 from our fifty overs – not a mountain, but a total with enough about it to make a game of it.

Defending it, we had Southborough's Gordon Baldwin to thank for most of the drama, an imperious 82 that had us checking the scorebook rather more often than we'd have liked, with Peter Young lending support on 25. Ron Kingsmill, mercifully, departed without so much as troubling the scorers, a small mercy gratefully received. It was Ron Hickman who held us together throughout, four wickets for 37 and nine maidens of positively miserly intent, with Brian Fuller (2-47) and Nigel Thirkell (1-11) doing their bit besides. My own 39 with the bat was, I like to think, a captain's innings, if only because nobody else was available to bowl it.

A word for Nigel Thirkell, whose solitary wicket eased him past Ray Howard into 19th on the club's all-time list – progress of the quiet, unglamorous sort that all cricketers understand. With Southborough closing on 140/7 from 44, neither side could force matters, and honours were shared. A draw, then, but an honest one – earned rather than settled for, and no bad way to spend a Saturday.

Paul Bowles was the chief architect of the carnage, running through them with 7 for 29, while Ron Hickman offered miserly support, 3 for 20 from nine maidens that must have felt like an eternity at the crease for anyone facing him. Poor Cliff Anscombe had a trickier time of it behind the stumps, 10 byes slipping past for the only blemish on an otherwise fine day's work.

There was a personal footnote too: John Ames's 54 eased him past Stan Wratten into third on the club's all-time run-scoring list, while John Thirkell (109) and Paul Bowles (20 wickets) continue to sit handsomely in this season's Midweek XI charts. A 75-run victory, and a very good day for the club indeed.
