🗓️ 26 Sep – 02 Oct 1970 · 1970 — 1 match

Sat 26 Sep 1970 🧢 1st XIv SC St Lukes Maidstone CC · HomeformLLDWW

From the captain's chair — Saturday 26 September 1970. St Lukes Maidstone CC were the visitors, and having called correctly at the toss, I had no hesitation in choosing to bat, the pitch looking far too good to leave to the openers of the other side.

And how the lads repaid that confidence. We ground our way to a handsome 227 for 3 from 46 overs, the sort of total that has you enjoying your tea rather more than usual. Basil Sears led from the front with a fine 54, John Ames matched him almost run for run with 52, and David T Lowen anchored things beautifully with an unbeaten fifty of his own. Three half-centuries in one innings is not something this reporter gets to write every week, and I shan't pretend I wasn't rather pleased with myself for winning that toss.

With the ball, however, we rather ran away with ourselves. St Lukes were bowled out for a mere 95 in 39.2 overs, E- Aiston's 29 and B- Pearce's 22 the only real resistance, while poor B- Lamb and Ron Hickman departed without troubling the scorers. Ron himself made ample amends with the ball, a magnificent 4 for 7 including five maidens — miserly doesn't begin to cover it — with Graham Mountford (4-45) and Mike Stephens (2-18) mopping up the rest.

A thumping 132-run win, then, and a thoroughly satisfying afternoon's work. Heads up, backs straight — and back to the nets we go, already looking forward to the next one.

Sun 20 Sep 1970 🧢 1st XIv HC Harrietsham CC · HomeformLLLDW

The lads gathered on a fine Sunday to welcome Harrietsham CC, and when the coin came down my way there was really only one decision to make — we'd bat.

We made a solid fist of it, posting 163 for 6 from our fifty overs. Paul Bowles played the innings of the day, a really fine half-century, with Mike Iliffe chipping in handsomely alongside him with 45, and Dick Langridge showing the youngsters how to finish an innings unbeaten on 22. I managed to add a useful 45 of my own. Not every name troubled the scorers mind — Brian Humphries and Jon Harris both departed for golden ducks, though I dare say they'll dine out on the story rather more than the scorecard.

Defending 163 always promises an afternoon of nerves, but the bowlers were magnificent. Paul Bowles, having already done his bit with the bat, ran through them with 4 for 12, backed superbly by David T Lowen's 3 for 10 and Dick Langridge's 2 for 51. Harrietsham's Laurie Holloway and Dennis Stones fought hardest with 24 and 23, but it was never enough, and they folded for 104. Poor Nigel O'Toole had a torrid time behind the stumps, gifting nine byes, but no matter.

A fine win by 59 runs — onwards indeed.
