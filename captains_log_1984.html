🗓️ 29 Sep – 05 Oct 1984 · 1984 — 1 match

Sat 29 Sep 1984 🧢 1st XIv HC Hastings & St Leonards Priory CC · HomeformLLWLD

We hosted Hastings & St Leonards Priory on a fine Saturday, and for once the coin let us down — they had first use of a pitch that, on reflection, suited us rather better with ball in hand. Alan Booth-Jones departed for a duck with just 7 on the board, and from there Jon Harris simply refused to let them settle, weaving through the order for 5-37 with three miserly maidens thrown in for good measure. Paul Bowles and Ian Brattle chipped in either side with two wickets apiece, and though C Watson's gutsy 27 kept the total respectable, 108 all out from 43.2 overs always felt like a target well within our compass. I even found some rhythm myself, picking up five, which I shall be modestly reminding the lads of for weeks to come.

With the bat, Bob Lines made light work of the chase, motoring to 70 not out and looking in no mood to give it back, while Brian Piper's unbeaten 28 provided watchful support at the other end. We eased home at 112/1 in 32.5 overs, wickets to spare and tea barely stewed. Poor Malcolm Pay had a torrid time behind the stumps, 8 byes escaping his grasp, though he'll no doubt point out the ball was doing plenty.

Two points, thoroughly earned, and a nine-wicket win to boot. With Bob Lines now third in the season's run charts and Jon Harris leading the wickets, this was as complete a team performance as we've managed all summer — bowled, batted, and byes notwithstanding, thoroughly good fun.

🗓️ 22 Sep – 28 Sep 1984 · 1984 — 2 matches

Wed 26 Sep 1984 🧢 Midweek XIv Penshurst Park CC · AwayformWWLWD

As the last cricket of a fine September evening at Penshurst Park, and toss called correctly, my instinct told me to bat — and for once, the instinct did not let me down.

The lads gave us something to work with, if not without the usual heart palpitations. Trevor Back's fifty (53) set the tone in fine style, and when Paul Bowles joined him for an unbeaten 53 of his own, their fifth-wicket stand of 80 was the innings' backbone. Jim Hall chipped in a useful 30. But cricket being cricket, we contrived to lose four wickets between 12 and 21 in the sort of collapse that gives captains grey hairs, and poor Tim Theobald departed for a duck without so much as a look-in. Credit, then, to a spirited last-wicket stand that bolted on 48 and hauled 177/7 from 48 overs into respectable territory.

Defending it, Penshurst threatened briefly through P Grayland's well-made 72 and A Robertson's 28, but an early wicket for nought set them back on their heels, and three of their number — Littlechild, Jenner and Boyden — went back without troubling the scorers. Paul Bowles, not content with his runs, turned his arm over to devastating effect: 6-39, the spell of the day by some distance, with Ian Brattle (3-37) and the miserly Jon Harris (1-40, seven maidens) mopping up. Nine byes did slip past Jim Hall's gloves, but nobody was counting by then.

A 48-run win, and Trevor Back climbs to 25th on the club's all-time run charts. Onwards indeed.

Sun 23 Sep 1984 🧢 1st XIv AC Anchorians CC · HomeformWLLWL

It was Anchorians CC at home on Sunday, and winning the toss I had no hesitation in taking first knock — the right call, I felt, and I'll take the credit while it's going.

A quiet personal footnote: those 18 runs of Jon's nudged him past Cliff Anscombe into 13th on the club's all-time run charts — not bad for a man who also leads our 1984 wicket-takers with 86. Paul Bowles, meanwhile, continues to bat like a man possessed, 1407 runs and counting.

A draw, then — honours even, pride intact, and the tea urn, as ever, the real match-winner.
