Sun 20 Sep 2015🧢 Sunday 1st XIv Tenterden CC · HomeformWLLWW

Sunday 20th September, a grey and rather agreeable afternoon at Linton Park, and we welcomed Tenterden CC in fine spirits. The coin behaved itself for once and I had no hesitation in putting us in — a decision that, for once, I can take some credit for. James Piper played the innings of the day, 90 well-earned runs that formed the backbone of our total, with Trevor Back (35 not out) and Kevin Presneill (30) offering sterling support. The openers gave us the perfect platform, 89 on the board before the first wicket fell, though poor Nat Presneill will want this one struck from the record — a duck to nurse on the drive home. A generous 27 extras rounded out a fine 236/5 from our 40 overs.

With the ball, we set about defending it with real purpose. Tim Thirkell was the chief architect of Tenterden's downfall, 2-13 and full of menace, with Trevor Back and Richard Knowler chipping in a wicket apiece — Knowler bowling with admirable meanness, four maidens to his name. Tom Richards (71) and Fred Pearman (20 not out) did their best to rescue things for the visitors, but 132/7 was never going to trouble our total. Nine byes suggested the gloves had a testing afternoon behind the stumps, and David Gare departed without so much as troubling the scorers.

A splendid 104-run win, then, and a day of individual milestones besides — Kevin Presneill and Tim Thirkell both climbing the club's all-time lists. Onwards indeed.

Sat 19 Sep 2015🧢 1st XIv Stowting CC · HomeformLWLWL

Saturday 19 September 2015, and the coin — for once — did what I asked of it. Grey skies over Linton Park, Stowting CC the visitors, and I put us in, on the theory that runs on the board beat runs in the chase every time.

We laboured to 159/8 from our forty overs, which felt distinctly middling as we walked off but would prove just enough. Thomas Beeney anchored things nicely with an unbeaten 40, James Piper chipped in a fine 36, and Joshua Knowler added 23 with the minimum of fuss. Richard Piper, alas, contributed nothing but company for the ducks in the pond — out for a golden nought, the sort of dismissal best forgotten over tea.

In the field, Stowting made a real fist of it, closing on 153/9 — Clifford Ball the chief nuisance with 48 — but George Sheen departed without so much as troubling the scorer. Darrell Houghton-Judge was superb with the ball, 3-38, well supported by Joshua Knowler (1-12, two miserly maidens) and James Piper (1-15). Poor Luke Welsman had a torrid time behind the stumps, 12 byes leaking away like sand through fingers, while I did my bit with 36 of my own.

Six runs the difference in the end — nerve-shredding stuff, but a verdict is a verdict. A very good day for the club, and one I'll happily take.

Sun 13 Sep 2015🧢 Sunday 1st XIv Cobham CC, Kent · HomeformWWLLW

We came out on top by 31 at home to Cobham, and if you'd told me at 6-1 that we'd be putting away a total north of 200, I'd have suggested a lie down. James Piper had other ideas, mind — a proper hundred, 115 of the finest, and when he was finally prised out we'd banked 213 for 4 off our forty. Glen Rawson chipped in with an unbeaten fifty of his own, and the pair of them stitched together 163 for the third wicket after that shaky opening, the sort of partnership that makes a captain's coin toss look positively inspired.

Cobham gave it a proper go in reply, closing on 182 for 9, with Benjamin Bowles top-scoring on 36 and useful runs from Joe Martin and Lucas Marshall keeping them in the hunt longer than we'd have liked. Mark Christmas took the new ball and the acclaim, finishing with 2 for 25, though 16 byes conceded on his watch rather took the shine off it — a testing afternoon behind the stumps if nothing else. Martin Harris and Benjamin Austin both weighed in with two apiece, and there was a small mercy for us in Callen Fahey departing for a duck.

Piper's 115 also nudges him past Stan Wratten into 19th on the club's all-time run list, which is the kind of thing that gets mentioned rather too often in the bar. All told, a damp Sunday, a good toss won and rather better used, and a thoroughly satisfying 31-run win to take home.
