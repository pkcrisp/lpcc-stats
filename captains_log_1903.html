Sat 29 Aug 1903 🧢 1st XIv Town Malling CC · HomeformDLDLL

Lost the toss again — I'm beginning to suspect the club coin has a grudge against me — and Town Malling CC, our visitors on a fine Saturday, elected to bat. No matter: it turned into rather a happy afternoon to be captaining from the field. F Reeves was in irresistible form, spinning his way to 6 wickets for none, the kind of spell that has batsmen playing for turn that isn't there and murmuring darkly on the walk back. Rev F A Leveson-Gower chipped in handsomely with 4 for none, and I'm told the skipper wasn't entirely idle either, picking up 4 wickets of his own. F B Leney and P Phillips both departed without troubling the scorers, and only W R Stratton, with a gritty 21, gave the visitors' innings any real shape before it folded.

With the bat we made hard graft look almost respectable. Rev H S Grant led the way with a fine 25, well supported by H G Smith (22) and Rev J R Leigh, whose watchful 20 nudged him past Leslie Wilson into 15th on the club's all-time run-scorers' list — a small piece of immortality earned the hard way. Extras chipped in a generous 29, for which we are, as ever, quietly grateful. Reeves's six wickets, meanwhile, lifted him past J Woollett into 13th among the club's all-time wicket-takers.

We ran out winners by an innings and 3 runs — a proper thumping, and just the tonic for a lovely August day.
