🗓️ 30 Sep – 06 Oct 1961 · 1961 — 1 match

Sat 30 Sep 1961 🧢 1st XIv SC Staplehurst CC · AwayformLLWDW

Skipper's Log — Saturday 30 September 1961, away to Staplehurst CC.

Winning the toss on a Saturday always puts a spring in a captain's step, and I had no hesitation in taking first knock — the right call, I felt, and for once I wasn't proved a fool by tea. Ron Aiston set the tone with a crisp 53, Tony Snook matched him run for run with a fine half-century of his own, and young J. Mills rounded things off in style, 52 not out and looking like he could have batted through supper. 180 for the loss of only two in 34 overs was as handsome a total as we've posted all season, and I confess I allowed myself a quiet smile at the tea interval.

The Staplehurst reply never quite got away from us, though P. Hodges gave us a fright with a determined 56, well backed by D. Thirkell and J. Lefevre. But wherever there was a threat, there was an answer — Jack Eastland and K. Hyde back in the pavilion for ducks, and Michael Chambers bowling beautifully for 3-34. Alan Tucker chipped in with 2-22 and a pair of maidens for good measure, and Terry Whittaker — who leads our wicket charts with 66 for the season — took 2-28 to keep the squeeze on. 156 for 7 from their 37 overs left them short, but not short enough for a win.

So honours shared, a draw wrought from a match we probably shaded. Not the result the batting deserved, but a thoroughly agreeable afternoon's cricket all the same.

Sun 24 Sep 1961 🧢 Marriedv Linton Park CC · HomeformLDLLL

There was needle in the toss this morning, and when it fell my way I had no hesitation — we'd bat. On a pitch that offered a little for everyone, 197 for 7 from our 42 overs felt like a total with substance to it, built on Robert Murray's watchful, well-paced 67. He was handsomely supported by John Thirkell's brisk 38 and John Crellin's tidy 29, while I contributed my own 38 down the order, mostly by running very hard between the wickets and hoping nobody noticed the shot selection.

Murray's innings deserves its own line in the ledger, mind — those 67 runs carried him past Frank Taylor and into 11th on the club's all-time run-scorers list, which is the sort of quiet, unglamorous achievement that this club runs on. He also leads our 1961 Married run charts on 67, keeping his nose just ahead of Robert Wood, who tops the Singles column with 58 — a friendly rivalry that, I suspect, will run and run through the autumn.

In the end neither side could force matters home, and honours were shared. A draw is rarely the stuff of legend, but with Murray in this sort of form and the rest of the lads chipping in around him, I came away thinking we're building something rather than merely surviving it. A solid day's work, then — no more, no less, and none the worse for that.

Sat 23 Sep 1961 🧢 1st XIv Bearsted CC · HomeformDLLWD

Saturday 23rd September saw Bearsted CC pay us a visit, and having lost the toss – a coin that has shown me precious little affection all season – we were invited to field first, which at least spared my ageing legs the indignity of an early declaration of intent.

The visitors compiled a respectable 169 for 7 off their 43 overs, built around a fine half-century from Ron Parsons (51) with good support from Colin Fleetwood (31) and George Lambell (29). It was Alan Tucker who did the real damage with the ball, a splendid 3 for 60, well backed up by Robert Wood (1-23) and Terry Whittaker (1-24) keeping things tight at the other end.

Chasing 170, we made light work of it, wrapping things up at 170 for 2 in just 32.1 overs. John Thirkell played an innings I shall be dining out on for some time – 128 not out, a superb, chanceless hundred that took the game clean away from Bearsted – with Robert Murray (37 not out) offering splendid support. Poor Robert Wood, so miserly with the ball, contributed a golden duck with the bat 🦆, proof that cricket rarely lets a man have it all in one afternoon. There was extra cause for cheer too, as Murray brought up 2,000 career runs for the club along the way.

An eight-wicket win, two points banked, and a thoroughly satisfying afternoon's work all round.

A draw, then, honours even, and on reflection a fair result — though with Howard in this sort of form, one wonders how much longer he'll be satisfied merely sharing them.

Sat 16 Sep 1961 🧢 1st XIv BC Bowaters CC · HomeformLLDLL

It was Bowaters CC at home, and having called correctly at the toss I had no hesitation in taking first knock — a decision I remain rather smug about. Two points, thoroughly earned, and a 34-run victory that flattered nobody — we simply outbatted, and then thoroughly outbowled, the visitors.

In the end, honours were shared — a draw, and frankly a fair reflection of an afternoon where neither side quite had the stomach to finish the job.
