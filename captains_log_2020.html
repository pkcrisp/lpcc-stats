Sat 26 Sep 2020 🧢 1st XIv The Mote CC · HomeformWWWLD

Saturday 26 September brought The Mote CC to Linton Park on a cool, damp, blustery afternoon — the kind of day where you can talk yourself into anything at the toss, and I talked myself into fielding first. I'll stand by it.

The visitors made themselves at home, racing to 216/6 from their 35 overs. Lewis Fawcett and Matthew White both eased serenely to unbeaten fifties, with Jason White chipping in with 49 for good measure — the sort of top order that makes a captain wish he'd stayed in bed. Small mercies came in the shape of Oliver Hills, back in the pavilion for a duck, and Alex Keeler, who did the real damage with 2-17. Tobey Castle (2-26) and Anthony Karassellos (2-58) lent honest support, though truth be told we were chasing shadows most of the afternoon.

Our reply never quite got going, folding for 112 in 27 overs. Luke Welsman's 30 and Jordan Austin's 26 were the only real resistance; everything else around them wilted rather too readily, with Richard Piper, Anthony Karassellos and John Karassellos all back for ducks. As for your captain — nought, and the least said about that innings the better.

Beaten by 104 runs, then, and no arguing with the scoreboard. There is a silver lining of sorts: Tobey Castle continues his fine season, second in the run charts and top of the wicket pile for 2020. For the rest of us, it's heads up, chins up, and back to the nets.

Sun 20 Sep 2020 🧢 Sunday 2nd XIv Meopham CC · HomeformWWLWL

Right by the fire, another season's page turned. Sunday, 20 September, Meopham CC the visitors, a warm and overcast afternoon that promised little but delivered plenty. I called correctly at the toss and, in a fit of uncharacteristic decisiveness, elected to bat.

The lads rallied to 161/7 from our 35 overs — nothing to have Meopham quaking in their whites, but a total with enough substance to work with. Henry Bourdillon led the way with a tidy 37, Fred Taylor chipped in handsomely with 35, and Benjamin Austin added a further 29. Not fireworks, but honest runs, gathered with the sort of quiet application that wins matches nobody remembers losing.

Defending it was another matter entirely. Meopham's Alex Bryant threatened to spoil the afternoon with a fine half-century (60), well supported by David Pollard's 36 and Andrew Bryant's 30 — for a while it looked ominously like their day. But Ben Pearce and James Innes departed without troubling the scorers, and Fred Taylor, following his runs with the ball, took 2-36 to lead the attack, with James West (1-11) and Anton Bourdillon (1-19) chipping away at the other end. Meopham fell agonisingly short on 160/8.

Won by a single run — the kind of margin that ages a captain by several years but tastes all the sweeter for it. Two points, thoroughly earned, and a reminder that in this game, one run is as good as a hundred.

Sun 20 Sep 2020 🧢 Sunday 1st XIv TC Tenterden CC · AwayformLLDWW

Another Sunday, another coin toss lost — I'm beginning to suspect it's rigged against me personally — and so off we trudged to Tenterden on a warm, overcast afternoon with the ball in hand rather than the bat. Not the worst hardship, as it turned out. Tenterden ground their way to 162 all out from 59.5 overs, with Mark Kokkinos looking rather too comfortable for our liking on his way to a well-made 51. Toby Hughes, evidently not content with his bowling heroics, carried his bat for a superb 78 not out, and found an able ally in Richard Piper (47) as the pair put on 78 for the second wicket to see us home in some style.

A word too for Anthony Karassellos, who brought up his 50th career wicket — a landmark worth raising a glass to — while Tobey Castle continues to lead our 2020 Sunday 1st XI run charts with 273 to his name. All told, an 8-wicket win, comfortably achieved, and a thoroughly satisfying afternoon's work. Onwards indeed.

Sat 19 Sep 2020 🧢 2nd XIv SC Sheldwich CC · AwayformWWWLL

Skipper's log, Saturday 19 September 2020: the fixture list took us out to Sheldwich, and, this being a village green rather than a health spa, I duly won the toss and stuck us in the field, on the grounds that watching is easier than batting when you've a dodgy back.

Sheldwich made their way to 118 all out in 36.5 overs, Jon Taylor's 21 the only knock of note, and full credit to the bowlers for keeping a lid on things — two maidens from Anthony Karassellos (1-6) suggests a man in no mood for charity. Tim Thirkell was the chief tormentor, 4-10 figures that will look rather fine in the club almanac, ably backed up by Ian Hollingworth's 2-23. Chase down 119 and we're home.

Which, eventually, in the manner of these things, we were — though not without the traditional degree of self-harm. Richard Piper's 35 anchored things nicely, Will Thew's 20 kept the board ticking, and I chipped in with 35 of my own, more by luck than design. James West's golden duck joins the Duck Club ledger, poor lad, and Martin Rivers had a torrid afternoon behind the stumps, 18 byes conceded telling their own tale. We limped over the line at 122/9 off 37.4 overs — won by one solitary wicket.

Not pretty, not restful on the nerves, but two points is two points, and Richard Piper and Tim Thirkell continue to lead the run and wicket charts respectively. A proper nerve-shredder, thoroughly earned, and one for the scrapbook.
