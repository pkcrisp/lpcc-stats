Sat 27 Sep 1969 🧢 1st XIv ? · HomeformLDLDL

Skipper's Log — Saturday 27 September 1969

Won the toss, and with the sort of confidence I'd later come to regret, elected to bat. It did not go well. We were bowled out for 100 in 43.4 overs, a total that flattered us more than it should have. Terry Donovan played the innings of the day with 30, and Mike Iliffe chipped in gamely with 21, but around them the wheels came off rather spectacularly — Terry Whittaker, Brian Relf and Jon Harris all took the long walk back for nought, and as for the skipper, well, a golden duck is not the example one hopes to set. Lead from the front, they say. I led from the pavilion steps back to the dressing room.

In the field we did our best to make a match of it, but M. Kendall had other ideas, cruising to 60 not out and taking the game away from us almost single-handedly. Jon Harris at least had the satisfaction of picking up 1-19, a lone bright spot with the ball. They closed on 101/1 in 22 overs, and the nine-wicket margin tells its own sorry story.

There was, happily, one thing to raise a glass to: Richard Ashby brought up 1,000 career runs for the club, a landmark thoroughly deserved and about the only thing that went right all afternoon. Paul Bowles, meanwhile, continues to lead our 1969 run charts with 921 — someone in this side knows what they're doing with a bat, even if it wasn't me this week.

Verdict: a chastening afternoon all round, but there's character in this side yet, and we'll be better for it next week.

🗓️ 20 Sep – 26 Sep 1969 · 1969 — 1 match

Sat 20 Sep 1969 🧢 1st XIv West Farleigh CC · HomeformWLDLD

Saturday 20 September 1969, and West Farleigh CC came calling — a fixture I'd circled, and one I'll be thinking about for a while yet.

Winning the toss, I had no hesitation in taking first knock, and for a good while it looked the shrewd move. Basil Sears was in fine, watchful form for his unbeaten 39, with Paul Bowles (32) and Andrew Moss (21) chipping in handsomely as we compiled 185 for 8 from our sixty overs — a total I felt gave us something to bowl at.

And bowl at it we did, magnificently in patches. Brian Relf was quite superb, tying the visitors down with three maidens on his way to 4 for 33, and had the satisfaction of sending John P Day back for a duck. Terry Whittaker (2-17) and Terry Donovan (2-26) did their bit too, and for long stretches West Farleigh were made to work desperately hard for every run. But Peter Cooper had other ideas, compiling a really fine unbeaten 66, and found a willing ally in Ron Kemp, whose own half-century (51) kept the required rate within reach. Between them they dragged their side to 186 for 9 off forty overs — and, agonisingly, home by a single wicket.

Paul Bowles continues to lead our 1st XI run charts this season with 911, and Terry Whittaker sits second among the wicket-takers with 54 — small consolations on a bruising evening. We were the better side for large parts of this one, and that, in the end, is cricket's cruellest lesson. We'll dust ourselves down and come back stronger next week.

I contributed a watchful 23 myself, while Keith Walker and R Pocock endured the walk of shame for ducks.

One wicket. That's all that separated triumph from ignominy — but a win, however scruffy, is a win, and two points thoroughly earned.
