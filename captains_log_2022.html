Sun 25 Sep 2022🧢 Sunday 1st XIv Crusaders CC · HomeformLWWWW

From the captain's chair, Sunday 25 September, and a pleasantly grey afternoon greeted Crusaders CC to Linton Park. I lost the toss — no great surprise there, my coin-calling remains as reliable as the Kent weather — and we were asked to chase, which meant an afternoon of leather-chasing before we'd even had our tea.

Crusaders batted with real purpose, closing on 269/4 from their forty overs. Sanam Pannu compiled a fine unbeaten 78, with Jin Singh's well-made 53 and Chand Channa's 42 doing plenty of the heavy lifting besides. Junaid Mufti was our pick of the attack, 1-19 and tidy throughout, with Nolan Wilde (1-30) and Sam Boyns (1-39) offering good support in what was otherwise an uphill toil in the field. Poor Sam also had a torrid time behind the stumps, 13 byes conceded — the sort of afternoon a keeper would rather forget.

Chasing 270, we never got close, bundled out for 120 in 32.5 overs. Theo Clements and John Karassellos both contributed golden ducks to the cause, which about summed up our afternoon with the bat.

Elsewhere in the season, Jordan Austin continues to lead the Sunday 1st XI run charts with 421, and Sam Boyns sits 4th among the wicket-takers with 11 — small consolations on a chastening day.

Down by 149 runs, then. Not our day at all — heads up, and back to the nets.

Sat 24 Sep 2022🧢 1st XIv The Mote CC · HomeformWLLLL

A pleasant, damp Saturday saw The Mote CC roll into Linton Park, and true to form the coin gave us nothing to cheer about — they won the toss and sent us in, no doubt eyeing those grey skies with quiet satisfaction.

We made a fair fist of it early on. A draw, then — honours shared, nerves spared, and the tea urn undefeated as ever.
