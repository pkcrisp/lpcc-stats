🗓️ 02 Sep – 08 Sep 1950 · 1950 — 1 match

Sat 02 Sep 1950 🧢 1st XIv BC Bowaters CC · HomeformDDDWL

Saturday 02 September 1950, and Bowaters CC came to Linton Park for what turned into a thoroughly satisfactory afternoon's cricket. I won the toss, elected to bat — the right call, I felt, and I'm not often wrong about these things, or so I tell myself — and we set about building something defendable.

We were bowled out for 148 in 44.2 overs, which perhaps flatters the batting more than the innings deserved. D Simmons top-scored with a fine 39, Arthur Cradduck was unbeaten on 30, and John Thirkell chipped in 24. Brian Relf and Bert Buckoke both departed for golden ducks, the sort of dismissals that give a captain grey hairs, though nobody mentioned it in the tea interval — much.

With the total posted, it was Thirkell's day with the ball as much as the bat. He took 7 for 19, four of those overs maidens, and simply strangled the life out of the Bowaters innings. H Newport chipped in with 3 for 29, and between us we sent Henry Martin, Stephen Jarrett and John Ibbotson back for ducks. Only William Hammond, unbeaten on 24, showed any real resistance as Bowaters folded for 48 in 20 overs. I managed 24 with the bat and, happily, 7 wickets with the ball myself — a rare day when the captain's contributions matched the team's mood.

A hundred-run victory, then, and a thoroughly good day for the club. Thirkell now leads our season's run charts and sits fifth in the wickets — the man is becoming something of a nuisance to opposing sides, and long may it continue.

🗓️ 01 Jul – 07 Jul 1950 · 1950 — 1 match

Sat 01 Jul 1950 🧢 1st XIv Bearsted CC · HomeformWLDDD

Another good toss lost, another happy outcome — you'd almost think I preferred it that way. Bearsted won the coin flip and elected to bat, which on a day like this felt like doing us a favour. Vic Pound played beautifully for his unbeaten 67, but around him his side simply fell apart: 7 for 1 early on set the tone, and before Bearsted knew what had hit them they'd slumped from 16 for 4 to all out for 99 in 35.5 overs. Gordon A Hawkins, Norman Tiley and John Colegate all trooped back without troubling the scorers, undone by a superb spell from John --'s Taylor, 4 for 47 with five maidens thrown in for good measure, backed up handsomely by C Horton (3 for 43) and Frank Taylor (2 for 5).

Chasing 99 should have been a formality, and for a while it looked it — 72 on the board before the first wicket fell. Then, as is our club's way, we conspired to make a meal of it, sliding from 106 for 2 to 109 for 5 in the time it takes to pour a tea. Poor Bert Buckoke contributed his golden duck to proceedings. But John --'s Taylor's fine 54, ably supported by Basil Sears's 30 — enough to see him climb to 14th on the club's all-time run charts — carried us home regardless.

A five-wicket win, then, earned the hard way after making the easy way look difficult. Onwards.

S Andrews and R Murrell both departed without scoring, and the bowling was tight throughout — Don Hartridge the pick with 2-19, John Thirkell and Stan Wratten chipping in either side, and John "-'s" Taylor squeezing out three maidens for good measure. Stan's solitary wicket, incidentally, was no small thing — it eased him past Herbert Sunley into 12th on the club's all-time list, which is the sort of statistic that will keep him insufferable at the bar for weeks.

In the end neither side could quite force the door open, and honours were shared. A draw, but an honest one — and on this evidence, the season is very much alive.
