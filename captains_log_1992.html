🗓️ 10 Oct – 16 Oct 1992 · 1992 — 1 match

Sun 11 Oct 1992 🧢 1st XIv TC Tenterden CC · AwayformWDWWW

Sunday 11 October found us on the road to Tenterden, and having called correctly for once, I saw no reason to be coy about it — up we went to bat.

It proved a shrewd call. Clive Newman set about the Tenterden attack with real purpose, finishing unbeaten on a fine 54, while Paul Bowles (45) and Nigel Thirkell (40) gave him sturdy company either side. Seventy runs were on the board before we lost so much as a wicket, and by the time our innings closed at 169 for 3 off 39 overs, even I had found the confidence to chip in with 40 of my own — a rare sighting, and one I intend to dine out on for some time.

Defending it, however, was another matter entirely. Rob Doick did his level best up front, prising out two for 36, with Ian Brattle (2-45) and the ever-parsimonious Alan Boyns (1-29, two maidens) chipping away alongside him. But P Knight had other ideas, compiling a magnificent unbeaten 87 that our bowlers simply couldn't shift, ushered along by John Oliver (21) and Spencer Gray (20 not out), whose fifth-wicket stand of 60 quietly did for us. Tenterden crept past our total at 171 for 5 with just three balls of the match to spare.

Not our day, then — beaten by four wickets, and a valuable lesson in never quite relaxing once the openers have done the hard work. Heads up, chins high, and back to the nets we go.

🗓️ 19 Sep – 25 Sep 1992 · 1992 — 2 matches

Sun 20 Sep 1992 🧢 1st XIv AC Anchorians CC · HomeformDWDWW

Sunday 20 September 1992, and Anchorians CC came to Linton Park to find the coin unkind to them — heads it was, and I had no hesitation in sending us in. What followed was a proper batting effort: Dave Charlton played the innings of the day with 78, well past his fifty, while David Castle's 37 and Nigel Thirkell's 25 gave it the substance it needed. Their 91-run stand for the fourth wicket was really the backbone of the whole afternoon, and 215/9 off our 37 overs felt like a total with a bit of bite in it.

Anchorians never quite got going in reply. An early wobble at 1-1 set the mood, and D Roberts departed for a golden duck to start the rot. Trevor Back was magnificent, tearing through with 4-40, with Rob Doick (2-23, three miserly maidens to boot) and Jon Harris (2-27) tidying up either end. J Bacon's 34 and useful contributions from Keith Wooding (27) and D Stubbs (25) were the only real resistance, and 151 all out from 40.2 overs left them well short. I even chipped in with 25 of my own with the ball, for what it's worth.

A word too for Piers McDonald, whose single run nudged him past Robert Chance into 25th on the club's all-time run-scorers list — immortality, of a sort, one run at a time.

Sixty-four runs the margin, two points banked, and thoroughly deserved. A day when everything we do well came off at once — I'll take that every Sunday going.

Sat 19 Sep 1992 🧢 2nd XIv RC Rainham (Kent) CC · AwayformLDWLW

Saturday 19 September 1992 — away to Rainham (Kent), and for once the coin actually behaved itself for me. I called correctly, and with a firm track underfoot there was never any doubt: we'd bat.

It didn't start well. Six on the board and we'd already lost a wicket, the sort of nervy little stumble that has openers muttering into their tea. But out of that wobble came the innings of the day: a third-wicket stand worth 133, built on Keith Hatcher's wonderfully controlled 98 not out and Brian Piper's fine 64. Piper's knock, incidentally, now nudges him past Tim Thirkell into 5th on the club's all-time run-scoring list — a lovely footnote to a fine summer's work. We closed on 213/6 from our 48, a total with enough about it to make Rainham work hard.

And work hard they did, without ever quite threatening. Stuart Cheesman was the pick, 3-36, with brother Fred chipping in with 2-15 and Rob Doick a wicket for 11. Dave Todd, meanwhile, was positively miserly — four maidens, not a run given away lightly. Rainham crawled to 139/8 off 41, McFarlane's 32 and Loader's unbeaten 30 the best of their resistance, but the follow-through simply wasn't there.

Honours shared, then — a draw when neither side could force the issue. Frustrating for the bowlers, no doubt, but with Piper etching his name into the record books and Mike Elworthy still striding out ahead in the season's run charts, there's plenty for the club to be cheerful about.

In the end we fell agonisingly short, beaten by two wickets, 223 not quite enough despite our best efforts. Peter Whyte and Jon Harris continue to lead the season's charts, which is some consolation. We'll be better for it next week — we usually are, eventually.
