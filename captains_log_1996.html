Sat 28 Sep 1996 🧢 2nd XIv Linton Park CC · HomeformDLWWW

Skipper's Log — Saturday 28 September 1996. Won the toss, and with the sun making a rare late-season effort, there was really only one decision to make. We batted.

218 for 4 off our 37 overs is the sort of total that has you allowing yourself a small, private smile at tea, and Stuart Cheesman was the man responsible for most of that smugness, cruising past his fifty to finish on 56. Dave Todd chipped in a fine 48 and Richard Piper 36, while your skipper contributed a modest 27 — enough to look useful without doing anything to trouble the record books. All told, it had the shape of a match-winning score, the kind of total you post and then quietly start planning what to say in the write-up.

Alas, Linton Park had other ideas, chasing us down with five wickets in hand and leaving us to trudge off rueing whatever it is one rues on days like this — a missed run-out, a dropped catch, the general unfairness of the universe. Elsewhere in the club, the season charts keep ticking along nicely regardless: Clive Newman sits fourth in the 1st XI standings on 778 runs, and Trevor Back fourth in the 2nds on 321, proof that somebody round here is doing something right.

Verdict: a batting performance to be proud of, a bowling and fielding effort to forget. Heads up, chins in, and back to the nets.

Skipper's Log — Wednesday 25 September 1996

Home advantage, a decent tea in prospect, and I won the toss and stuck them in — a decision I stand by, even if the scorebook makes for slightly bracing reading. The visitors settled in nicely for 185/6 off their 45, built around a well-made fifty from H. Zaltzman, with N. Keest (30) and an unbeaten 20 from D. Smith padding things out. Credit where due: Darren Styles bowled beautifully for his 3-26, Stuart Cheesman chipped in with 2-12, and Trevor Back was a model of parsimony — 1-7 and three maidens, not a run given away lightly.

Chasing 186, we simply cantered — 189/3 in 26 overs, the game more or less put to bed by tea. Peter Whyte played a proper captain's innings of 86, well supported by Mike Elworthy's fine 67, and I rattled along to 16 of my own. Whyte had a tricky afternoon behind the stumps mind you, 30 byes going begging — we'll say no more about that.

One footnote for the club historians: Elworthy's 67 nudges him past Cliff Anscombe into 23rd on the all-time run-scorers list, while David Castle (2nd, 138) and Stuart Cheesman (3rd, 9 wickets) continue to climb this season's Midweek XI charts.

Verdict: runs a-plenty, wickets shared about, byes notwithstanding — a satisfying afternoon's work, and one to build on.

Sun 22 Sep 1996 🧢 1st XIv AC Anchorians CC · HomeformDDLLL

Here's the match report:

There are moments in a captain's season when the coin gods smile, and Sunday last was one of them. Anchorians duly arrived, I called correctly, and – ever the optimist, or possibly the pessimist about our bowling attack – I put us in.

We built something rather handsome: 175 for 5 off our 40, with David Castle at his fluent best for 55, Clive Newman keeping him honest with 49, and Matt Large finishing unbeaten on 24 to give the total a bit of late gloss. A total, I felt, with genuine teeth in it.

Alas, teeth can be blunted. Anchorians replied with the sort of assured innings that makes a captain rue every field placing, reaching 178 for 4 in the 41st over. M Claydon's unbeaten 58 and Eddie Stanford's 55 did most of the damage, and no amount of huffing from the boundary was going to stop them. Darren Styles at least gave us something to cheer, snaring 2 for 18, with Fred Cheesman and Michael Beeney chipping in a wicket apiece – Beeney's two maidens a small monument to discipline amid the carnage. I contributed a modest 24 with the bat, which felt rather beside the point by the close.

Not our day, then – beaten by six wickets, and no dressing it up otherwise. Still, David Castle sails on at the top of the season's run charts with 1046, and Darren Styles sits third among the wicket-takers with 24 – reminders that the campaign has plenty of substance beneath Sunday's disappointment. Heads up, chins out, and back to the nets.
