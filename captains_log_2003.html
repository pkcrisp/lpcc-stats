Sat 27 Sep 2003 🧢 1st XIv Sherwood CC, Kent · AwayformWWDLD

Saturday last took us down to Sherwood CC, Kent, and I confess I won the toss and stuck us in without much agonising — the pitch had that look about it. Not for the first time, the decision paid handsomely. Sherwood huffed and puffed their way to just 66 all out in 27.3 overs, with only Tim Newlyn's watchful 20 giving the innings any shape, while poor Michael Hooker trudged off for a duck. Ian Brattle was the chief tormentor, a superb 4-18 that had their middle order in all sorts of trouble, with Adam Jackson (2-18) and Russell Baldwin (1-0) mopping up either end, and Dennis Salisbury bowling with the parsimony of a man checking every receipt — four maidens and barely a run conceded.

Chasing 67 was never going to trouble us on such a morning, and Richard Piper (27 not out) and Rob Jackson (23 not out) simply strolled to the target inside 16 overs without so much as a alarm, let alone a wicket lost.

Worth a mention in the clubhouse: Brattle's four-for eased him past John Ames into 6th on the club's all-time wicket-takers list, while Dennis Salisbury continues his quietly ruthless season, now 2nd in the 1st XI's 2003 charts with 23 victims.

A comprehensive nine-wicket win, then, and precious little for me to do but applaud from the boundary. Onwards we go.

Wed 24 Sep 2003 🧢 Midweek XIv Penshurst Park CC · AwayformDWDWD

Wednesday last, the road took us to Penshurst Park, and the toss went the way tosses so often go for us this season — badly. Asked to bowl first, I can't say my heart sank, not with Darren Styles marking out his run. Penshurst posted a handsome 218/6 from their 38 overs, built on a fine half-century from P Eames (68) and matched stroke for stroke by David Smith, also 68, with R Hunter chipping in 45 further down. Darren was our chief tormentor with 3-36, well backed up by Jon Harris (2-21) and Trevor Back (1-33) — a proper team effort with the ball, even if the total looked a good one over tea.

Chasing 219, we could have been forgiven a few nerves, but Richard Piper had other ideas, compiling a magnificent unbeaten 107, with Craig Sands matching him run for run on an unbeaten hundred of his own. Between them they simply took the game away from Penshurst, ushering us home in 34.2 overs for the loss of just two wickets. The one blemish on an otherwise gleaming card was poor Brian Piper, undone for a duck — these things happen to the best of us, Brian, chin up.

Craig now leads our 2003 run charts with 408, and Jon Harris tops the wickets with 15 — form continuing nicely for both. An eight-wicket win, then, and a thoroughly satisfying afternoon's work. Onwards.

Sun 21 Sep 2003 🧢 Sunday 1st XIv AC Anchorians CC · HomeformWWWDD

From the captain's chair — Sunday 21 September 2003, and we welcomed Anchorians CC to Linton Park on a fine Sunday for it. Winning the toss, I had no hesitation in electing to bat, and for once the decision looked every inch the masterstroke it felt like at the time. Pask's 36, and Anchorians strolled home on 225/2 with 32.5 overs gone. Michael Beeney did what he could up front with 1-34, John Taylor chipping in with 1-58, but the pitch offered little sympathy for honest toil.

Beaten by seven wickets, then, and no arguing with the placing. Thirkell's century was a joy, Beeney continues to lead our wicket charts and Sands the run charts, and we go again next week rather better for the lesson than the scoreline suggests.
