Sat 22 Sep 2018🧢 2nd XIv Cudham Wyse CC · HomeformLWLLL

22 September 2018, and the visitors were Cudham Wyse CC — a cool, damp afternoon that felt like it might swallow the runs whole. I won the toss and, rather against the grey skies' advice, chose to bat. It was, if I say so myself, the right call.

Luke Welsman set the tone with an unbeaten, well-crafted 53, and he wasn't short of company — Jordan Austin chipped in with 30 and Charles Curteis added an unbeaten 30 of his own, the openers putting on a crucial 52 for the first wicket before either of them had so much as loosened their shoulders. We closed on 155 for 3 from our 22 overs, a total that felt substantial enough to defend even before a ball had been bowled in reply.

And defend it we did. Abishek Pushpavinothan was quite simply magnificent, ripping through their order for 4 for 18, with John Karassellos (1-17) and Tobey Castle (1-20) chipping away either side of him. Cudham Wyse slid from 23 for 1 to 37 for 4 in the blink of an eye, and though they steadied themselves to close on 91 for 6 from their 20 overs, the damage was long done.

In the end neither side could force it home, and honours were shared. A draw, then — but one where we did rather more of the winning than the drawing.

Sun 16 Sep 2018🧢 Sunday 1st XIv Benenden CC · HomeformWLLLW

Sunday 16 September 2018, and Benenden CC came to Linton Park on a warm, damp afternoon that suited nobody's swing bowling. I won the toss and, in a rare fit of decisiveness, stuck us in to bat. Archie Fairfax-Ross and Tobey Castle set about proving me right with half-centuries apiece — 65 not out and 63 — while Aaron Hughes chipped in a handy 36. The openers gave us the platform we needed, 64 for the first before anyone troubled the scorers with a dismissal, and the tail, not to be left out of the fun, wagged for a defiant 74 at the death. Thirty-seven extras arrived gratis, for which we offer Benenden our sincere and entirely insincere thanks. Two hundred and ninety-eight for six felt like a very good afternoon's work indeed.

Defending it, we let their bowlers drop short early on and rocked back to punish it square — a lesson, it turned out, we'd forgotten to teach ourselves with the ball, as our own too-short stuff got carted off the back foot all afternoon. Andrew Morrison threatened to make a match of it with 67, backed up by Rob Willetts' unbeaten 34, including a shot of the day that sailed clean over mid-on for six. But Abishek Pushpavinothan (2-26) and Darrell Houghton-Judge (2-33) kept chipping away, Toby Allsworth strangled the middle overs, and a horror 24th over — two wickets down in a flash — did for them. Poor Aaron had a torrid time behind the stumps, 14 byes going astray, but with the bat safely banked he could afford to laugh it off. A thoroughly earned 119-run win, and two points nobody can argue with.

Wed 12 Sep 2018🧢 Midweek XIv Penshurst Park CC · AwayformLLDLW

Skipper's Log, Wednesday 12th September 2018: away to Penshurst Park CC, under skies that couldn't quite decide whether they were crying with us or for us. Ducks for Mark Alexander, Jake Cheesman and Steven Alexander did the damage, with Sam Boyns the pick of the attack — 2-10 with two miserly maidens — well supported by Ryan Boyns and Luke Welsman, one apiece.

As for your skipper, a golden blob — leading by example is all very well, but not, apparently, with the bat in hand. Credit where due: A McDonald's six through mid-wicket was the shot of the day and the only moment of genuine class either side mustered.

We scrambled home by four wickets, ragged but unbowed. A win's a win, and this club has never been too proud to take an ugly one.
