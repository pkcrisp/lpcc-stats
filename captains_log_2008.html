Sat 27 Sep 2008 🧢 1st XIv CC Chelsfield CC · HomeformLLLDD

Saturday 27th of September saw Chelsfield CC roll into Linton Park, and after losing the toss we were asked to have a bowl first — no complaints from me, I do enjoy watching other people do the running about. Chelsfield settled in nicely and posted 227/5 from their 39, built around a fine innings of 85 from C Martin and well supported by U Hassan's equally handsome 53, with X Stuary unbeaten on 49 to round things off. Sam Boyns was the pick of our attack with 2-32, Chris Newman and Michael Beeney chipped in with a wicket apiece, and Richard Knowler bowled two overs so tight you couldn't have posted a letter through them.

Chasing 228 was always going to ask questions of us, and truth be told we didn't quite have the answers. Tim Thirkell played the innings of the day with a battling 38, Richard Piper supported nicely with 27, but we subsided to 147/8 from our 39 overs. Kevin Presneill's golden duck was matched, I'm sorry to report, by your own captain's effort — I shall draw a discreet veil over the details and simply say the walk back to the pavilion felt considerably longer than the walk out.

So honours shared, a draw when neither side could quite land the killer blow. Piper's runs keep him handily placed in the season's charts, fifth with 181, while Beeney's wickets have him fourth overall on 13 — proof, if any were needed, that there's real substance in this side even on days when the captain contributes rather less than nothing.

Sat 20 Sep 2008 🧢 1st XIv SC Sidcup CC · HomeformLLLLD

From the captain's chair — Saturday 20 September 2008, and Sidcup CC in town for what turned into a proper old-fashioned arm-wrestle of a draw.

No luck with the coin, and Sidcup wasted no time making us pay for it, rattling along to 248/2 from their 38 overs. David G Rolls was the chief tormentor, an imperious 102 not out, with Kim O'Mara's well-crafted 77 and Stephen Shanks' 42 doing plenty of damage either side of a 129-run stand for the second wicket that had us chasing our tails in the field. Tim Thirkell was the one bright spark with the ball, snaffling 1-11 off a probing spell, with Richard Knowler chipping in for 1-45.

Chasing 249, we made a proper fist of it. James Piper produced an innings of real substance — 105, no less, and along the way brought up 3,000 career runs for the club, which rather puts the rest of us to shame. Nigel Thirkell gave him fine company with a well-made 70, the pair adding 126 for the fourth wicket to drag us right back into it. But cricket, being cricket, could not resist a late twist: from 227-5 we stumbled to 228-8 in the blink of an eye, Matthew Carter enduring the walk of shame for a duck along the way.

So honours shared, neither side quite able to land the killer blow. Piper's ton keeps him second in the season's run charts, and with Knowler still leading the wickets column, there's plenty to be cheerful about — even in a draw that felt, frankly, like a win we let slip through our fingers.
