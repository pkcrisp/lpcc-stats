🗓️ 30 Sep – 06 Oct 1995 · 1995 — 1 match

Sat 30 Sep 1995 🧢 2nd XIv YC Yalding CC · HomeformWWWWL

Won the toss, elected to bat, and for once my judgement on the day was vindicated rather than merely tolerated. David Castle and the openers gave us the platform every captain dreams of and few deserve — 152 for the first wicket before the breakthrough finally came — and from there Castle went on to 81, Mike Caddick weighed in with a well-made 70, and Mike Elworthy finished unbeaten on 29 as we declared ourselves satisfied with 226 for 4 from our 42 overs. Tea was taken in good spirits, as well it might be with that on the board.

Defending it should, in theory, have been the easy part. Yalding stumbled early — 1 for 1 set a tone we were only too happy to encourage — and we sent Myers, Tony Shepherd and Simon Quirk back without a run to show for their trouble. Only Iain Cardew, unbeaten on a defiant 58, threatened to make a match of it before they folded for 107 in 49.4 overs. Paul Bowles led the line beautifully with 3 for 28, Alan Boyns chipped in 2 for 0, and Jon Harris was miserly as ever — 2 for 12 with five maidens that must have felt like a slow death to face. Caddick, admittedly, had a trickier time of it behind the stumps, with eleven byes escaping his grasp.

Milestones aplenty too: Castle reached 12,000 career runs for the club, and Caddick's 70 eased him past Mike Iliffe to 25th on the all-time list. A fine afternoon's work — won by 119 runs, and two points thoroughly banked.

🗓️ 23 Sep – 29 Sep 1995 · 1995 — 1 match

Sat 23 Sep 1995 🧢 2nd XIv HC Higham & Mockbeggar CC · HomeformWWWWW

Saturday last, and Higham & Mockbeggar came calling to Linton Park with the sun still holding and the square looking a picture. I called correctly at the toss and never once considered bowling first — some habits die harder than others, and putting the ball down before us on a good pitch has never sat easily with me.

We laboured to 130 all out in 37.3 overs, which tells its own story, though Mike Elworthy batted like a man who knew exactly why he was there, cruising past his fifty to 65 and dragging the total to something respectable almost single-handed. The rest of us, it must be said, offered rather less resistance.

In the field we started brightly enough — Jon Harris bowling five miserly maidens and Dave Todd striking first with 1-22 — but Colin Robson and P Davis put on 81 for the first wicket before we so much as sniffed a breakthrough, and by the time N Scudder finished unbeaten on 27 the game had long slipped from our grasp. Beaten by seven wickets, no arguing with that.

Still, a day with real cause for pride: Mike Elworthy brought up his 200th appearance in Linton colours, a landmark few of us will match, and sits second in the 2nd XI run charts with 354 to his name, with Dave Todd third among the bowlers on 14 wickets. The result stung, but the club goes on. We'll be better for it next week.

Sun 10 Sep 1995 🧢 1st XIv CC Crowborough CC · HomeformLWDDD

Sunday last, we welcomed Crowborough CC on what turned out to be a proper afternoon of village cricket, and I have to say I called the coin rather better than I usually manage — heads it was, and bat it was too, without a flicker of hesitation on my part.

We made a fair fist of it, 155 for 5 off our 43, built on a splendid opening stand of 73 before Crowborough finally prised us apart. David Castle led from the front with a well-crafted fifty, the sort of innings that reminds you why he sits third in the club's run charts this season with 873 to his name, and he had good company: Mike Elworthy chipped in a handsome 42, while young Daniel Thirkell finished unbeaten on 29, playing the innings of a man in no rush to go anywhere. I'm told the captain also had a hand in matters with 50 of his own, though I shall let others do the boasting on my behalf. Spare a thought, though, for poor Richard Ellis, whose golden duck was the one blot on an otherwise sunny scorecard — these things happen to the best of us, and I dare say he'll have his revenge before the season's out.

In the end neither side could force the issue, and honours were shared. A draw it may have been, but with a start like ours and Castle in such rude form, I'll happily take it as a job well done.
