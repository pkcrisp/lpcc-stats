Sat 17 Aug 1940 🧢 1st XIv CA Charles Arkcoll · AwayformWWWLL

Saturday last took us to Charles Arkcoll, and the coin, as is so often its habit with me, landed the wrong way up — they won the toss and chose to bat, leaving us to make do with the ball first. No great hardship, as it turned out. They were bowled out for 110 in 23 overs, K Heathfield top-scoring gamely with 45, but the middle order simply melted away from 24-2 to 40-5, with D Jolliffe, G Wratten and X Heard all trooping back for ducks. J Short was the chief tormentor with 3-12, well supported by Herbert Rayner's 3-33 and Don Hartridge's 2-20.

Chasing 111, we made distinctly harder work of it than the target deserved, stumbling to 112 all out in 27.5 overs. I contributed a scratchy 17 with the bat and, rather more pleasingly, 3 wickets with the ball — a rare afternoon of being useful at both ends.

Two small footnotes for the record books: J Short's 19 runs eased him past Leslie Wilson into 20th on the club's all-time run-scorers list, while Bert Buckoke's solitary wicket nudged him past J N Tonge into 15th among the wicket-takers. Percy Brattle continues to lead the 1940 run charts with 474, and Herbert Rayner the wickets with 32.

A scruffy, nerve-jangling win by 0 wickets, but a win nonetheless. Onwards.
