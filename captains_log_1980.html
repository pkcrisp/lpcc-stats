Sat 27 Sep 1980 🧢 1st XIv Lordswood CC · HomeformWDDLD

Saturday 27 September 1980, and Lordswood CC came calling. Won the toss and, in what I'd like to call a bold statement of intent rather than mere habit, stuck us in first.

It was not our finest hour with the bat, truth be told. 141 all out from 50.3 overs, the innings unravelling rather briskly from 126-7 to all out. Nigel Thirkell held things together with a well-crafted 52, and got useful company from Andy Woollett and Trevor Back, both 23. Special mention, as tradition demands, to Martin Thompson, who contributed a duck of such quality it will live long in the ledger, if not the memory.

Lordswood's reply gave us a fright or two, reaching 114 before folding in the 42nd over. Steve Marsh's 55 was the innings of note, backed up by Terry Tocknell (23) and K Darlington (20), and for a while it looked ominous — until Jon Harris, in his 300th appearance in our colours (a landmark achieved, fittingly, with the ball rather than the bat, though his 5 runs did creep him past Mike Iliffe to 17th on the club's all-time run list), ran through them with 5-36 and six miserly maidens. I chipped in with five of my own, and Nigel Thirkell — who leads both our run and wicket charts this season, greedily hoarding both honours — took 2-21 for good measure.

We got home by 27 runs. Scrappy with the bat, splendid with the ball, and a thoroughly good day for the club — Jon's 300th could hardly have been better marked.

Sun 21 Sep 1980 🧢 1st XIv AC Anchorians CC · HomeformDWDDL

Sunday 21 September brought Anchorians CC to Linton Park, and having called correctly for once, I saw no reason to complicate matters — up we went to bat.

It would be kind to call our innings modest. 105 all out in 45.5 overs is the sort of total that makes for a long tea interval and longer faces, though Paul Bowles did his level best to disguise the wreckage with a fighting 35. Poor David Iliffe, meanwhile, contributed nothing at all to the cause — a golden duck, and not the first he's collected, I'll wager, but never a pleasant one to walk off with.

In the field, though, the lads found their mettle. Anchorians reached 102 for 7 from their 32 overs, Paul Stanford top-scoring with 39 and Derek Ufton chipping in a solid 34, the two of them putting on a stand of 56 that threatened, for a while, to bat us clean out of it. R. Durbridge came and went for a duck of his own — one suspects he barely had time to take guard. It was Nigel Thirkell who kept us in the contest throughout, a fine 4 for 31, with Trevor Back chipping in 1 for 23 and Tony Gauna bowling two miserly maidens to choke the runs.

In the end honours were shared, neither side quite able to force the issue — and amid the toil, Paul Bowles quietly raised his 13,000th career run for the club, which is rather more memorable than anything on the scorecard. A scrappy draw, then, but a proud one.

Sat 20 Sep 1980 🧢 1st XIv West Farleigh CC · HomeformDDWDD

West Farleigh arrived on a fine September Saturday, I won the toss, and — against all better judgement, some might say — elected to bat. I stand by it. What followed, alas, was not our finest hour with the willow: 63 all out in 39.2 overs, a total that took its time dying but died all the same. An early wicket at 8-1 set a nervy tone, and then, with the grim inevitability of a British summer turning to rain, the innings collapsed from 34-4 to 42-7 in the time it takes to pour a tea. Quite how we stretched that to 63 I still can't entirely explain — stubbornness, mostly.

Defending such a modest total was always going to be a tall order, and so it proved, though not without pride intact. Ron Hickman bowled with real discipline — four maidens and 1-15 don't lie — while Nigel Thirkell was, as he so often is this season, our pick of the attack, snaring 2-26. But West Farleigh's Roger Crooks made hay with 29 of the bulk, and they eased home at 64-3 from 21.5 overs, winning by seven wickets with overs and dignity to spare.

Thirkell continues his extraordinary season regardless — 1265 runs and 74 wickets in the 1st XI charts speaks for itself, one-man band that he is. As for the rest of us: a batting collapse to forget, a bowling effort to be proud of, and a defeat that stings just enough to sharpen us for next week.

Wednesday 10 September 1980, and off we trooped to Penryn St Gluvias, where the toss decided to go my way for once and I promptly decided we should bat — no point standing on ceremony when the coin's finally on your side.

Nigel Thirkell was quite simply magnificent, six wickets for 27 and six maidens to boot, the sort of miserly, relentless spell that has him sitting proudly atop both our run and wicket charts for the season. Paul Bowles chipped in with two more, Ross Brice with one, and Phillip Smith's 35 and G Stephens' 31 were the only real resistance as Penryn folded for 107 in 34.4 overs.

Poor Fred Greenfield had a torrid afternoon behind the stumps, eight byes going begging, but no matter — we came home 36 runs to the good. A thoroughly satisfactory day's work, and one Nigel Thirkell can rightly call his own.
